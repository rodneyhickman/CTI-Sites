    The Struggle

    The hedgehogs struggle to understand the meaning of the note. They inspect the device, and take turns manipulating its moveable dials and symbols, but without success. As time passes, the young hedgehogs grow increasingly frustrated and fearful; an argument breaks out over who controls the object. The largest, most vocal hedgehog steps forward and snatches the note from one of the others and begins to tear up the paper. “I’ll handle it — we don’t need this!” he screams.

    “Wait,” another says, grabbing the paper back. “We might.”

    In the uneasy silence that follows, each starts thinking about what he or she has learned that might be “necessary.” Some wonder about special talents or gifts they might have. One of them knows he is an excellent climber, another a fast runner; one of them is a powerful burrower; the largest one, quiet now, thinks of his skill as a builder. None sees how he can help the group reach the other side.

    Discovery of the Code

    The smallest hedgehog works on the device. Turning and lining up dials, she moves three unconnected circles together so that they overlap. In their intersection a word magically appears: “ME.” In that moment, the small hedgehog has an insight: The group’s success is up to her. She shares the discovery with the others, and sees the same look of insight cross their faces — and almost as quickly sees the determined look that comes with responsibility. They look back at the strange device, and the letters magically form a new word: “WE.”

    In a flash of inspiration, the hedgehogs move into action, together. The best climber, using three strong hedgehogs as a ladder, scales a nearby tree, and sees in the far distance the reason for the chasm: a massive earthen dam holding back life-giving water from the dry, deep channel. The swift runner lifts the burrower on his back and takes off upriver, to do the work of burrowing holes in the dam. The builder gathers the others around him and says they need to find something they can bind together and use as a sturdy raft. A voice from the back says, “Use us.”

    A New World

    Trickles streaming through the burrowed holes in the dam become torrents, and in a rush the dam turns into a magnificent waterfall, releasing a powerful flood that races down through the dry chasm. The water level rapidly rises. The hedgehogs forming the raft link arms and legs. The others hop on, and hold on. Shooting down the rapids, every one of them feels alive, powerful, changed.

    Far downstream, the raft washes up on the opposite shore, where rivulets of water are almost instantly bringing soil to life. The land is more fertile and full of possibilities than any of the young hedgehogs had ever before imagined.
