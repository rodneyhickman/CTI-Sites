    THE BALANCE FORMULA

    As you remember from your classroom experience, the Balance Formula consists of five steps. In a typical coaching session, you might cover all of the steps in the Balance Formula or you might spend a whole coaching session on any one step.

    Review: Balance Formula

      » Perspective — how do clients view this topic or area of their life?
      » Choice — clients have the power to make resonant choices based on life purpose and values.
      » Co-Active Strategy — determines the direction of choice and is based on gut, heart and mind functioning as one.
      » Commitment — a declaration, “This will be.” Committing to the strategy with what you are saying yes and no to.
      » Action — we are reminded that the real action of coaching takes place in the client’s life, not in the coaching session accompanied by accountability.

    For more information on the new Balance Formula go to The Balance Formula At A Glance.

    What’s New: Planning Becomes Co-Active Strategy

    The first thing you will notice is that Balance Formula has slightly changed. “Step 3: Planning” has been renamed to Co-Active Strategy. The Co-Active Strategy has two parts: The “Co” and the “Active”. Most planning and strategy is only the Active part. It leaves out foundation of the Co... so we have people in action that is not connected to anything. Co-Active Strategy has clients connect action to resonance.

    The “Co” is space, the essence out of which form is born. The “Active” energy is about forward movement, power, strength, action, direction, focus, movement, decisiveness. Together, these play a much larger role than Planning.
