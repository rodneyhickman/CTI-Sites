    THE CO-ACTIVE MODEL


    What’s New: Changes to the Model

    We have redesigned the “Co-Active Coaching Model” and have renamed it the “Co-Active Model”. We have found that the Co-Active Way applies across the board to all human beings. This change begins with our foundation, The Four Cornerstones.

    What’s New: The Four Cornerstones

    You will be working with this new version of the Model as you go through the rest of your courses so please get familiar with it. The changes are to the layout of the Model and the Four Cornerstones, so focus on them as you go through the content.

    » People are Naturally Creative, Resourceful and Whole. By changing this cornerstone from “clients” to “people”, it applies to everyone.

    » Dance in This Moment. Dance in “the” moment felt like a concept of coaching work we do in the future. We changed “the” to “this”. In reality, all people dance now in the magic of the present moment.

    » Focus on the Whole Person. This cornerstone is not territorial; not focused on who is showing up. It focuses on how we bring all aspects of the whole person into our coaching.

    » Evoke Transformation. “Holding the client’s agenda” has changed to “evoke transformation”. Holding the client’s agenda is now a skill we use to evoke transformation. We hold this as follows:

    • To evoke is to call forth.
    • We define transformation as a deep, elemental shift in consciousness on the path of evolution.

    At its most resonant, life is a journey of growth, evolution and transformation. People are yearning to be called forth into the greatest expression of them possible. They are hoping that someone will believe in them enough to stand for that. This change puts the coach back in the model.

    You can learn more about the new Model and Cornerstones at this link The Co-Active Model on the Fundamentals Learning Hub.
