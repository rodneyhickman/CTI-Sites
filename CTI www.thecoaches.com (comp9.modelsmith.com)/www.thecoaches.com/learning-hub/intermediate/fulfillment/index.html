    FULFILLMENT COURSE WORK

    FULFILLMENT COACHING IS ABOUT ALIVENESS. YOU WILL LEARN TO HELP CLIENTS TO LET GO OF PRECONCEIVED RULES AND LIMITATIONS AND TO STEP INTO WHAT TRULY BRINGS THEM ALIVE. 

    THE CORE SKILL OF FULFILLMENT COACHING IS THE ABILITY TO ACCESS YOUR (AND YOUR CLIENT’S) INNER WISDOM. IN THIS COURSE, YOU WILL DEVELOP A RELATIONSHIP WITH YOUR WISE INTERNAL VOICE AND LEARN TO DISTINGUISH BETWEEN THAT VOICE AND YOUR SABOTEUR.

    Please note:  Use of CTI Intellectual Property.  CTI students may use materials presented during their course work, including materials on CTI’s Learning Hub, in their work with their coaching clients only.  Any use of CTI Intellectual Property beyond one-to-one coaching is not allowed without the specific written approval of CTI.

    At the end of the course, you will be able to:

      » Use a model of inner dialog to assist clients in accessing the full range of their ability and power.
      » Assist clients in identifying their unique values and assessing their satisfaction with living those values.
      » Identify self-defeating influences.
      » Begin creating the relationship with a new client.
      » Conduct an initial Discovery Session.
      » Work with a client to clarify their life purpose.

    Before your course

    Before your course, browse through this website and get familiar with the material. Pay special attention to the areas listed below. The preparation may be done during the course if you will have access to the Learning Hub in the evenings of Day One or Day Two of the course.

    Click on the Resources link and review the Business Resources area, also review the Discovery Session Checklist and Discovery Session Template in the Tools area. This is in preparation for creating a rough draft of your own Discovery Session. Please make notes of your questions and any items you may want to include in your rough draft and bring your notes to the course.

    If for any reason you are unable to complete the preparation and won’t have access to the Learning Hub in the evenings of Day One or Day Two of the course, please plan to do the reading shortly after course completion.

    During your course

    Be willing to quiet your mind to be open to the whisper of your own deep internal wisdom and also to distinguish and confront the voice of your Saboteur.

    Set up a client for your practice coaching session on the evening of Day Two of your course.

    Arrange your life so that you can dive in and have your attention fully on the course and the experience during your three days.

    Do your homework.

    After your course

    Developing coaching skills is all about practicing. Here are some ways you can practice before your Balance Course:

      » Choose five Powerful Questions from the examples in the  Tools section of the Learning Hub and practice using them with your clients this week.
      » Enlist four people to become your practice clients. Charge them a nominal fee. Tell them you are in training to become a professional coach. Meet with them once a week for half an hour by telephone.
      » Practice your Discovery Session on two people and get feedback. What did they learn about themselves from this process? What do they recommend you keep in the Discovery Session? What would they cut from it? What would they add?
      » Use the Captain and Crew Chart from the  Tools section with at least two clients.
