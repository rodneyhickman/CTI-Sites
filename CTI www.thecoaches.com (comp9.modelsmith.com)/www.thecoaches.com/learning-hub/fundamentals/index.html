    Your Course Leaders

    One of the first things you will notice as a participant in the Co-Active Coaching Fundamentals Course is that there are two course leaders instead of one. Next, people often notice is that the presentation style of the two leaders is more like a collaborative dance than two separate leaders each presenting a selected portion of the agenda.

    At CTI, we believe that every individual brings a unique combination of gifts and talents to their coaching and leadership. With the Co-Active model, we are not aiming to produce “cookie-cutter” coaches. Our goal instead is to provide an adaptable model that provides both a solid philosophical foundation along with room for each participant to incorporate their own expression. In the co-leadership of your two course leaders, you will see a working example of the dance we believe is central to effective coaching.

    All of our course leaders are Certified Professional Co-Active Coaches (CPCCs) meaning they have completed all of the CTI coursework including the six-month intensive certification program. Many are Professional Certified Coaches (PCCs) through the International Coach Federation or Master Certified Coaches (MCCs).

    Outcomes

    The purpose of this course is to provide training and practical experience in the techniques and skill of the coaching profession, specifically Co-Active Coaching. This course is oriented toward participation and practical experience rather than lecture. You will have extensive opportunities to coach and to receive feedback on your coaching.

    As a result of taking this course, you will learn to:

      » Use the four cornerstones of Co-Active Coaching with your clients.
      » Communicate with your clients using the five contexts of Co-Active Coaching.
      » Use the Wheel of Life with clients.
      » Distinguish and use the three levels of listening in coaching and in your life.
      » Demonstrate the most often used coaching skills.
