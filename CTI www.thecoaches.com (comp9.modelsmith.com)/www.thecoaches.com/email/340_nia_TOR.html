During 2013, in the Toronto Co-Active Community, we are focussing on the whole person by taking each learning event to individually attend to mind, body and then spirit.

This second event is all about body, movement and what we can access from that sometimes underutilized and yet very important part of our coaching and personal instrument. In actuality, Nia is about using the body to integrate body, mind and spirit.

During this interactive session you will:

  • Experience the Co-Active model through dance and movement
  • Have the opportunity to listen to your body and it’s wisdom
  • Be introduced to Nia; a life practice that combines dance arts, healing arts and martial arts
  • Experience the synergy of Nia and Co-Activity in concept and community

This session of exploration is open to anyone, any age and any movement experience. It is sure to put an added spring in your step and beat in your heart.

What is Nia?
Nia is a fusion form of movement that combines the precision of Martial Arts, the creative expression of Dance Arts, and the functional fitness of Healing Arts. Nia emphasizes creativity, conditioning through community  and individual exploration. With a philosophy rooted in cultivating awareness, experiencing the joy of movement, and celebrating uniqueness. Nia delivers a fun, effective and powerful life practice for all ages and movement experience levels.

Facilitators

Deborah Colman loves to laugh and believes that when we cultivate joy in our lives we are better able to embrace the darkness in life with deep presence and resilience. As a seasoned businesswoman and executive coach, Deborah is committed to humanizing our connections and cultivating organizational cultures in which leaders think in possibility and excel in authentic and purposeful leadership. Deborah is a CTI Senior Faculty Member and Certified Co-Active Designer. She leads the Co-Active coaching core-curriculum to emerging coaches and seasoned Human Resource professionals and consultants, and provides organizations with customized talent management programs.
She is widely recognized by her clients, peers, and students alike as an inspiring and wise leader who generates transformative learning and sustainable results. Prior to becoming a coach and trainer, she worked in the public healthcare industry as an organizational development consultant and physical therapist.
Martha Randall is a passionate teacher who loves to share in the creative possibilities that movement provides for expression, discovery and self-healing. Martha’s 35 years of experience as a movement artist and teacher ensure that the classes and training sessions she leads are thoughtful, energetic and playful. Throughout her movement career starting with formal dance training at York University, Martha has studied and performed an eclectic range of dance forms, including modern, ballet, contact improvisation and Gabrielle Roth’s 5 Sacred Rhythms.
Martha’s teaching positions have included: Soul Pepper Theatre’s Youth Outreach program, Second City’s educational division, the Toronto Board of Education’s Conference for Music and Drama teachers and the Haliburton School of the Arts Expressive Arts Program. She has also volunteered Nia classes at Gilda’s Club – a support service for women with cancer.
