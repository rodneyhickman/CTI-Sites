No Brown Wrapper Required

Have you been reading escapist books all summer? There’s a new book, just released, that you won’t feel compelled to hide from hotel housekeeping. No embarrassing double-takes from people you pass at the airport. The book is called The Stake: The Making of Leaders and it is a creative exploration of the world of leadership, as experienced by five participants in a yearlong leadership training program. This might be the perfect book to wrap up your summer reading season.

Co-authored by CTI co-founder Henry Kimsey-House and faculty member David Skibbins, The Stake explores why today’s world requires men and women who can lead from their vision, passion, and deepest sense of purpose. The book explores how these new leaders call forth the resourcefulness and creativity of those they lead and hold a deep belief in their own capacity to change their world for the better.

The poet and political activist Muriel Rukeyser once wrote, “The universe is made of stories, not atoms.” Henry and David both love stories, and realized that stories were the magic element missing from the leadership literature. So they decided to wrap a fictional story around truth, and also to weave in true stories from the graduates of CTI's Co-Active Leadership Program.

The result is a compelling, readable book about how to train and develop powerful, responsible leaders.

“The Stake shows us that each of us is a leader and guides us on a journey of discovering and developing that leader inside us all. This is an important book, placing the responsibility for leadership in the hands of everyone rather than just a select few. A must-read for anyone who is committed to personal and professional transformation.”
—KEVIN CASHMAN, Bestselling Author, Leadership from the Inside Out and The Pause Principle; Senior Partner-CEO and Executive Development, Korn/Ferry International

Pick up your copy of The Stake: The Making of Leaders or learn more about the book at TheStakeBook.com.
