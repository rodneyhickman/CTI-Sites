Coaching the Forbidden and Taboo

What can’t you be with in your coaching?

I’m thrilled we are asking this question this month! Sure, we’re looking for what the client can’t be with in their life, but what about our hard stuff and the role it plays in the coaching relationship? In my work with my husband, Craig Carr, to develop Danger, Sex and Magic™: Coaching the Forbidden and Taboo, we’ve identified “danger areas” and are sharing new ways we’ve found to navigate through them. Rather than stepping over golden opportunities that scare us at first blush, we want to see coaches courageously take on the vibrant challenge of “taboo-busting,” and be willing to move with clients into the great depths of the unknown.

I have listened to hundreds of coaching hours in supervision over the years, and the most glaring thing I see related to what coaches can’t be with occurs when a client’s life experience exists outside the realm of the coach’s belief system.

Here’s an example of what I mean: A client came to a call very much wanting to talk about her sexual relationship with her partner. (The coach had done a great job training her about our whole life focus!) The coach proceeded to masterfully side-step the conversation by picking an accountability from the last call and managed to keep the coaching there for the entire session. When I asked the coach what happened, she very pointedly said, “There’s no way I was going to go there. I don’t want to talk about things like that! I don’t have conversations like that with anyone, let alone a client!”

So let’s be clear about what happened here. The forbidden territory they were in had nothing to do with the client—it was all the coach’s taboo stuff! Remember, when done well “a Co-Active coach does whatever it takes” (Synergy!), and creating a coaching conversation that is alive and resonant has within it the capacity to be very intimate and very vulnerable for both coach and client. The powerfully designed coaching relationship asks that both participants go to deep levels of openness, and then forward that trust and awareness into realms of unknown territory. In the above example, the coach’s decision to not explore the territory had tremendous impact on the relationship and the range of the coaching.

Craig and I feel this conversation is so vital to coaches that we have started a blog at http://web.mac.com/jilwindsor/iWeb/Site/Home.html, and we invite you participate in the conversation. Come and share your taboo adventures, stumbles and successes, and we will share our insights and support you on your journey.

My guess is that if you have been coaching for a while, you have had a few experiences in which your palms go clammy, your heart pounds, your head buzzes, your mouth goes dry and all sense of knowing seems to fly out of the window! If that sounds like you have arrived in what we call the “Land of Taboo,” you would be correct. In Danger, Sex and Magic (DSM), we feel this “Danger Zone” is an area that exists in and out of coaching, and we are constantly being called forth to acknowledge it. We want you to trust that it happens for a reason and that you are fully equipped to not only handle these moments but to look forward to them and love the adventure. We believe that in these precious moments we are being awakened, and we’re sure many of you are aware that this is one of the primary reasons you chose to be a coach in the first place.

In DSM, we devised the “Wheel of Generative Energy” to identify eight primary areas where these Danger/Taboo dynamics tend to show up. The energies are: Vulnerability, Magnetism and Attraction, Sacred and Divine, Passion and Desire, Body Awareness and Sensuality, Playfulness and Delight, Creativity and Sexuality. (For expanded definitions go to the glossary at http://web.mac.com/jilwindsor/iWeb/Site/Home.html. Click Glossary to learn more.)

Now think of a client or client situation. The first one to come to mind is probably the perfect one—and the hardest! With all degree of honesty and on a 0-10 scale, write down the “Generative Energies” and rate the degree each energy is being expressed in this coaching.

…Wasn’t that fun? How many qualities are below a 6 and how many are above? Do the exercise with a few more clients in mind and look for patterns. Which generative energy seems to be most excluded from your coaching? Which ones do you rely on most? Now, let’s take this a step further. Apply the Generative Energies to yourself to get a better understanding of your danger and taboo areas. This explores which qualities are getting short shrift in your own life and the cost to your aliveness. One thing we know for sure is that what you do or don’t do in your life will be re-created in the coaching relationship. It has to; it’s a natural phenomenon.

Now that you recognize where some of your forbidden and taboo areas are located, it’s time to take action that will include them and stop them from running your coaching (and your life!). Do this: pick one of the low-rated Generative Energies and write out a list of ways you could express it. Yes, this will start the energy of Forbidden and Taboo. Pay attention; get to know how it feels. Listen to internal dialogue; feel the resistance. Now pick three ways from your list and share them on the blog. You know what comes next—DO IT!

I look forward to you joining us to continue this rich discussion. There is much to be learned here for all of us. Your work as a coach is precious and a gift. Your clients and their lives are precious. Thank you for being in this incredible generative dance of becoming.

With love, Jil
