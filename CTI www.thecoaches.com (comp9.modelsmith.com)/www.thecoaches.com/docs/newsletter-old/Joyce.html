Staff Profile Joyce Wong

Joyce Wong is CTI’s Customer Service/Operations Administrator—say that 10 times in a row real fast! Her lovely voice greets many a new student, and her behind-the-scenes work keeps our whole office running smoothly. In her own words (and revealing her passion for cooking), Joyce says, “If CTI were a meatloaf, I’d be the breadcrumbs.” She holds us all together, not only in her sub-role of CTI mother, but in her operations role as well.

Joyce was born on Tuesday, December 15, which she says, “is a long, long time ago in a galaxy far, far away…,” though ironically Joyce is actually a San Francisco native and was born in St. Francis Hospital in the heart of San Francisco.

The first job Joyce ever had was working for her dad at the family grocery store, stocking the shelves. She was 8 years old at the time. While in high school, she worked in a warehouse for a retail department store. After college, she did a stint as a health educator for a family planning clinic. Eventually, Joyce made her way to The Sierra Club, where she worked in Member Services under the guidance of Jim Patterson. When Jim moved on to CTI, he contacted Joyce when CTI needed some administrative support. At that time, she had just decided to go back to work part-time after being a stay-at-home mom for three years, so it worked out perfectly.

At CTI, Joyce works very closely with the database and making sure the information gets inputted and uploaded for our courses and student records. She provides backup support for student registrations, and post course administrative follow-up for our international and corporate course offerings. She says, “That means I have lots of piles of paper on my desk!”

Joyce’s favorite aspect of the job she does is talking to different people each day, and having the connections with students and leaders. Her favorite thing about working at CTI is the staff. She enjoys knowing that the staff is really happy about working for CTI. She also enjoys the great location and the free parking! She’s an ESFJ on the Myers Briggs Personality indicator and says that, “Being an extrovert helps when I talk to so many people every day.”
 
The biggest aha Joyce has discovered about herself since she started working at CTI is that she doesn’t have to beat herself up if she fails, but she also knows that no one is better than her when it comes to bottom lining, getting things done, and seeing different perspectives. The things she can’t quite get the hang of?  Figuring out how to set the postage meter and un-jamming the printer!

Joyce brings her job home in more ways than one. She does work part-time from home, but she also loves to be Co-Active whenever she can. She says, “I love talking to different people in different places and learning about their lives. I love being Co-Active with my family, especially my nieces and nephews, who, I’ve been told, consider me the ‘cool Aunt.’” She also knows how to relax at home. When Joyce has completed a demanding project, she likes to celebrate by sitting in front of the TV with her latest knitting project and getting lost in a movie.
 
The last and most important thing to know about Joyce is that she is an Eccentric on the “I Am” typing scale, which means she enjoys being different. Could that be why Joyce says, “I have a warped sense of humor”? We think so, and we love her for it. She makes us laugh!

  1. If you could coach anyone in the world (living or dead), who would you coach  and why? I don’t know if I would coach him, but I would love to have a chat with Anthony Bourdain, chef, author of Kitchen Confidential and food traveler.
  2. What is your favorite food/recipe? Traditional Cantonese Chinese comfort food. It’s what I ate growing up, and what I crave when I’m sick or under stress. What food do you despise? Liver…YUCK!
  3. Most people don’t know that I…am addicted to cheesy Harlequin romance novels. Bubble gum for the brain.
  4. What are you often times found doing at 4 p.m. on any given Saturday afternoon? Standing in my kitchen figuring out a new recipe or out and about enjoying San Francisco.
  5. What is the most glorious moment/accomplishment of your life? There are two…having my daughter and traveling to London by myself.
  6. Who's your hero? My dad, who wasn’t afraid to travel the world even though he never learned to speak any other language than Cantonese.
  7. Where is your favorite place to play? With my family on a houseboat on Lake Shasta with a fishing pole in one hand and a romance novel in the other.
  8. What would we find at your desk? A picture of my daughter with the first fish she ever caught, a statue of “Bernard” (one of the wild things, from the book Where the Wild Things Are by Maurice Sendak) and my Easter Island Head tissue dispenser with the tissue coming out of its nose. And lots of different piles of paper.
