Staff Profile—Angie Rome Gonzalez

 

Angie Rome Gonzalez is the woman with the lovely voice and face that first greets anyone who calls or comes in person to CTI headquarters in San Rafael. She’s been with CTI for going on three years now and has touched many, many lives with her deep caring and concern for student welfare. Angie is a blessing in all of our lives and oversees this office with much love and care. It’s time that we all got to know her a little bit better, so look for the new things that you didn’t already know about Angie, and then call in and say hello! She’s always happy to hear from you.

Angie was born an Aries on March 24, 1963, in New Orleans, LA. She’s mostly Cajun-French, with a little bit of German thrown in (thus her maiden name “Rome”).  Her first job ever was stacking shoes at a Payless Shoe Source store in New Orleans. She was 16 years old and took the job to feed her “theatre addiction”—acting classes, university film series, costume pieces, etc. She left New Orleans in 1990 to come out to California for a Shakespeare costuming job in San Jose. She worked many different jobs before coming to CTI, including selling half-price theatre tickets in Union Square in downtown San Francisco, where she cut her customer service teeth. She eventually came to CTI through an ad on Craig’s List for “Manager of First Impressions,” a nice way of saying “telephone receptionist.” She had years of experience in customer service and a mom who is a life coach. She basically wrote in her cover letter that she was the “perfect fit” for this job. CTI agreed and the rest is history.

Angie started out in 2003 being said “Manager of First Impressions” and was promoted to Certification Specialist not long after. She filled that role for almost two years, then last August had her first baby. Upon her return to CTI following her maternity leave, she chose to go back to the job that she had first held in an effort to achieve work/life balance for herself. “Connection” is in her top five values; therefore, she loves talking to students and having the connection that happens on the phone. There is an additional reason that Angie is so good on the phone. In her own words: “Someone told me in one of the courses that I was a capital E (as in extrovert) in bright red neon lights with glitter all over it. I think how that affects my work persona is pretty obvious!”

She also says, “I love working for a company that embraces its values in such a tangible way—we list them on our website, we read them at our staff meetings, we explore them together as a staff and we live them to the best of our ability.”  It was Angie who started the tradition of reading the values at our meeting. (She doesn’t necessarily like to toot her own horn, so we’re going ahead and doing it for her!) It’s been a very valuable thing for all of us on staff, and we thank her kindly.

After Angie has completed a big project, she likes to celebrate and relax by going to a celebratory dinner at Chili’s, complete with margaritas and loved ones, followed by a long soak in a lavender-scented bath. She says nobody is better than her when it comes to connecting with people and organizing a shelf, but she still can’t quite get the hang of finding work/life balance since having her baby eight months ago. Her biggest aha since beginning work at CTI has been her love of coaching and the coaching courses. She says, “Thanks to the learning that I have had as I trained to become a CPCC, I’ve realized that FEAR had been a motivating factor in my life for every decision I ever made—good and bad. Being able to recognize the effect that FEAR has on me and being able to step through it courageously is the greatest gift I’ve received from CTI.” She applies what she’s learned about Co-Activity in the courses by listening and really hearing what’s going on around her in a very active way. Having a little person at home who cannot communicate with words has really helped her hone the skill of hearing what’s not being said. “I find this skill serves me in all the aspects of my life—with coaching clients, with co-workers and with family members.” 

  1. If you could coach anyone in the world (living or dead), who would you coach and why? I’d love to coach Peter Jackson. That’s a man who dreams big—really BIG—King Kong-sized BIG. I love being around big dreamers and getting to be a part of their journey.
  2. What is your favorite food/recipe? Anything Cajun, Mexican, Indian…basically anything HOT, HOT, HOT. What food do you despise? Carrots.
  3. Most people don’t know that I…can pattern a bustier, tailor a suit, sew a welt pocket and put in a lap zipper. All because I was a theatrical costumer for 14 years prior to going into customer service.
  4. What are you often found doing at 4 p.m. on any given Saturday? Changing a diaper.
  5. What is the most glorious moment/accomplishment of your life? Celestina Catherine Gonzalez, born August 15, 2005.
  6. Who's your hero? My Mom, Peggy Doucet Rome, who overcame loss, regret and fear to step into a magnificent life.
  7. Where is your favorite place to play? I love backyards with swing sets and patios and barbeque pits. A place where family can gather.
  8. What would we find at your desk? Photos of my family, a Louise Hay calendar, a calculator, computer, files, pens and, of course, the PHONE!
