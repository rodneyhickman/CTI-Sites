How to Increase Your Income by Changing Your Relationship with Money

Relationship Coaching is not just for relationships with other people. How about your relationship with your career? Your body? Aspects of yourself? More and more, I find myself coaching clients on their relationship with Money.

My story:

Until April of 2003, I was struggling with my coaching practice. I was having trouble attracting clients who would pay the fee I wanted. I found myself avoiding discussions of Money as long as I could. I was embarrassed by the whole subject, and my discomfort translated into making clients uncomfortable, too. I was "doing" all the right marketing things—networking, newsletters, sample sessions—and getting nowhere. I was not making a "grown-up" living.

What was in my way, I wondered? My coach and I took a look at my relationship with Money. What were my stories about Money? What is this entity I'm in relationship with? What's going on with this relationship?

Two discoveries popped out: Money didn't feel safe or reliable, and Money caused separation. (My family would swing between being rich and poor over and over again, and Money was a "reason" for family members not to talk to each other for decades.) If my experience of Money were given personhood, he'd look like an unappealing, unclean, Hell's Angel biker type that I didn't want to be around...someone untrustworthy who liked to cause fights. No wonder I wasn't bringing Money into my life!

This was not the relationship with Money I wanted to have. (And it wasn't the relationship I wanted to model for my clients, either.) So I created a new paradigm. I fired the biker persona and put a romantic, soft-spoken suitor in his place. I chose a new Money "person" to relate to. This Money was like a sweet boyfriend who wooed me with gifts. He even wore a tux! Whenever I received a check, signed a new client, came across some unexpected income, I would graciously thank Money for the lovely gift. And this version of Money was valued and invited into my life.

From then on, my business and income kept growing. Within six months, I had accrued such a waiting list of clients, I had to add group coaching to my services. I didn’t have to look for my new clients; they were finding me. And all I had changed was my inner dialogue with Money.
