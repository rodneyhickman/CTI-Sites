Staff Profile Stephanie Lovinger, CPCC

This month’s profile focuses on the lovely Stephanie Lovinger. Stephanie is one of our admissions advisors here at CTI, but she is also the resident bunny lover and humor provider for all of us here in the office. She is a Humor type on the I Am typing scale and that is very clear to all of us. If you hear lots of giggling and/or outright belly laughs coming from a particular place in the office, chances are Stephanie’s irreverent humor is at work! Things just wouldn’t be the same around here without her. Let’s take a closer look at who she is and how she came to join us at CTI.

Stephanie was born on October 18 in Flint, MI. She says, “If you’ve seen Michael Moore’s Roger and Me, you’ve experienced it” (Flint, MI, that is). She then adds, “And I’m not telling the year.” Her first job ever was as an ice cream scooper at Baskin-Robbins 31 Flavors. She wore a bright pink dress and pigtails, was 13 years old and was paid half the minimum wage, plus ice cream. (What a deal!) Though she only got half the minimum wage, she did get a great workout. She says, “My right arm got really strong!”  (Wait, that’s only half the deal too. Hmmm…)

After strengthening her arm at Baskin Robbins and several jobs later (including a number of sales and training positions for companies such as Discover Card, Communispond and Morgan Stanley), Stephanie eventually made her way to CTI. “I got my CPCC in 2002. I always told myself that if a part-time position came up at CTI that could use my skill-set, I’d jump on it. Then one day an email announcement came out to all CPCCs about the admissions advisor position. Of course, I had to call.”

Stephanie is now part of a team of three admissions advisors whose role is to serve as part coach and part advisor. When a prospective student has questions about our programs, they talk with an admissions advisor. She says her number one priority is to help students figure out if this is the right program for their Big “A” agenda.

When asked what her favorite aspect of the job is, Stephanie said, “Two things. First, no call is ever the same as another. I like variety, and it’s a brand new person on the line every time. Because of that, I get to use all of the skills in the Co-Active model. From Level 2 and 3 listening, to curiosity, to blurting my intuition. My job is just chock full ‘o Co-Activity!” Stephanie is an ENFP on the Myer’s Briggs Personality type indicator. The E is great for taking with people all day long. It also helps to create her outgoing and vivacious personality.

Her favorite thing about working at CTI? Stephanie loves her co-workers. In her own words: “I love my peeps! This organization is full of fascinating people. It’s also a great place to experiment at developing my strengths and being ‘leaderly.’ There is so much permission to be who you are and contribute from there.”

She also loves to spend time with co-workers outside of the office, especially when celebrating the completion of a big project. Sometimes she’ll go with some of the staff to Celia’s, a local Mexican restaurant. She finds that the margaritas there are quite refreshing. Other times she likes to sit on her new patio at home and just say Ahhhhhh.

The biggest aha she’s discovered about herself since starting working at CTI is that people actually rely on her to bring fun, energy and play into the workplace…and that it really is necessary. In addition, no one is better than her when it comes to loving bunny rabbits. She says, “I just can’t get enough of these cute little creatures. Now that everyone is on to this, I have been gifted with scads of bunny-related paraphernalia, from a pez dispenser to a bunny-of-the-month calendar.”

The thing she still can't quite get the hang of? Sitting still for too long.
 
When asked about Co-Activity, Stephanie says, “I use it all of the time in a million different ways. One interesting thing I’ve noticed is that my own brand of creativity has really opened up for me since 2001, when I started my CTI training. Coincidence? Maybe, but I don’t think so.” Another recent opening for Stephanie is that she just started teaching her favorite exercise class. It’s called the Bar Method. “I’ve been a student for years, and I’m finding that I absolutely love teaching it. Who knew? It feels like this realization is leading me to my next coaching niche.”
