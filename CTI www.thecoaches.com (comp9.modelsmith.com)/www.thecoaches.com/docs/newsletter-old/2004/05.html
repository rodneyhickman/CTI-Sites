CTI-UK

We are delighted to announce that we have a very enthusiastic and experienced team to lead the new CTI-UK.  Together they represent a vast amount of proven experience in spreading both co-active coaching and leadership.  In this transition, they have been generously supported by Linda Taylor and Stuart Durrant (Estrella Associates) who have done such a brilliant job spreading the fire of co-activity in the UK.  Linda and Stuart have been true pioneers in our community who have put CTI on the map in the UK through their sheer drive and commitment.  They partnered closely with CTI to choose their successors to ensure a bright future for the precious legacy they leave behind.  Please join us in wishing them great success in the new endeavors they will take on after a much needed and deserved vacation.

And now a word from our new CTI-UK partners:

We are honoured that CTI and Estrella chose us to hold the torch of co-active coaching for the next stage of its journey! Linda and Stuart from Estrella poured four years of love, care, attention and sheer hard work into opening up the UK and Europe to the impact and power of co-active coaching.

As many of you already know, we share in their passion, enthusiasm and commitment. Our team is headed by Martyn Lowesmith, Zoë Windsor and Andy Denne who between them have been co-active coaches for over 13 years. Like Linda, Martyn and Andy were members of the inaugural UK group who were all trained back in July 1999 by Laura Whitworth and Phil Sandahl. We (Martyn, Zoë and Andy) continue to run our own highly successful personal and corporate coaching practices and bring with us many years of business experience to help move the impact of co-active coaching in the UK to the next level.

It is our intention that this big fire of transformation that we are all building across the world continues to burn brightly and spread its reach further in the UK in all walks of life. In the UK co-active community, as elsewhere, there is an amazing vibrancy passion and conviction that we have all contributed to and built. And it is ready and hungry for more impact and more momentum. As a team, we are here to support and co-actively create, nurture and call forth new ways of making that happen for us as individuals and as a group.

To that end, we are committed to: creating initiatives to stretch, challenge and support existing and future co-active coaches in the UK; extending the range of opportunities for co-active coaches; increasing the availability of both public and corporate courses; and much, much more!

Whilst we are carrying this torch we are here to co-create the future of co-active coaching in the UK. We would love you to contact us with any comments, inputs or ideas that you have! 

Thank you for all of the support and good wishes that we know you are sending us. And we are looking forward to meeting and being in conversation with many of you over the coming months.

Relationship Systems Coaching Program

New Eligibility Requirements

In response to the interest from many coaches who are working with teams, families, organizations, couples, business partners, etc., we have changed our requirements for entry into the Relationship Systems Coaching Program making the courses available to you earlier in your coach training.

The requirements to take the Relationship Systems Coaching Fundamentals Course remain the same; you need to have completed 100 coach training hours with CTI or another accredited coach training program.

You are eligible to take the Advanced Courses of the Relationship System Coaching Program…

  • as soon as you are participating in CTI's Certification program, OR
  • as soon as you have completed 100 coach training hours from another accredited coach training program and 100 hours of coaching. 

In the past, we have required that you complete CTI's Certification program before taking the Advanced Courses of the Relationship Systems Coaching Program. 

Additional Locations

We are also expanding the number of locations where we offer Relationship Systems Coaching to include the Chicago and Minneapolis areas starting this summer.  The Relationship Systems Coaching Fundamentals course is being offered in Chicago on July 10, 2004 and in Minneapolis on September 11, 2004.  The remainder of the course series, the Advanced Courses of the Relationship Systems Coaching Program, will be offered in Chicago in the fall of 2004.

To register for a Special Information Call for the Chicago/Minneapolis Relationship Systems Coaching Series on Tuesday, June 1, 2004 at 4:00pm for, call The Coaches Training Institute at 1-800-691-6008 or visit www.thecoaches.com.

 

CTI's CEO at the Commonwealth Club

Shirzad Chamine, CTI's CEO, will be moderating an upcoming panel discussion at the Commonwealth Club of California, America's oldest and largest public affairs forum. The topic will be the current education crisis and how businesses can effectively get involved.

Due to the enormous budget cuts in education, businesses and educators are faced with a crisis in how they will be preparing the workforce of the future. A dramatic increase in offshore outsourcing for qualified employees is further highlighting the urgent need for this collaboration. The panel will explore best practices in companies getting involved in K-12 and higher education. It will have a vibrant dialog about the greatest opportunities for powerful alliances between business and education.

This panel discussion presents an ideal venue to also explore the potential role of coaches in transforming education. The panelists range from a prestigious economist specialized in this area, to corporate executives, to a school-to-career expert, to a high school director.

Tuesday June 1, reception at 5:30 p.m., Program at 6:00 p.m. San Francisco, California. Tickets $15. Call the Commonwealth Club at 415-597-6700.

 
 
Article

Professionally Speaking...

By Judith Cohen, CPCC, MCC

Webster's Unabridged Dictionary first defines profession as "an open declaration, public avowal, acknowledgment or claim". Lastly, it defines profession as "the occupation to which one devotes oneself, a calling in which one professes to have special knowledge used by way of either instructing, guiding or advising others or in serving them in some art as the profession of teaching."

It takes guts to make an open declaration, a public avowal that people deserve to live fulfilled and balanced lives AND that coaches can help people achieve this very lifestyle. It takes even more courage to publicly declare and avow that each person is a magnificent being, born naturally creative, resourceful and whole. What vulnerability, optimism and commitment this profession demands of us! Co-Active coaching is not for the faint of heart. Co-Active coaching is an active and constant professing of values and stands that support our clients' deepest desires and most powerful actions.

What is this special knowledge that Co-Active coaches maintain? It is our deep belief in our own naturally, creative resourceful and whole selves. It is a faith born of experience with both success and failure.  It is our own willingness to fully live into our Big "A" agendas no matter what circumstances confront us, no matter how loud our gremlins are shouting. And most importantly, it is the constant co-creation of our relationship with our own coaches, the individuals who support our desire to achieve a fulfilled and balanced life.  Read the article.

Henry Kimsey-House on Play:

Come Alive!  The Artful Play of Coaching. 

I have been looking at what distinguishes a competent coach from a masterful one. Of course experience plays a huge part, as it often takes time to move into that unconscious competence. The other quality that I have noticed is that a masterful coach knows how to play. They know how to play themselves and they know how to have their clients play. By play I don't mean just doing the up and fun stuff; I also mean able to play with the stuff that is hard and not so fun. So I got together with an expert in Play, Gwen Gordon, and we have put together a workshop that trains people how to play and trains coaches how to play with their clients.  (Gwen teaches play at the Institute for Transpersonal Psychology and the California Institute of Integral Studies.)

I have prided myself in the fact that CTI's workshops have always been filled with play and are highly entertaining as well as skill building.  With this workshop, we are now teaching others how to create this play for themselves and their clients.

I playfully invite you to join Gwen and me in this playground of masterful coaching.  June 29-July 1 in San Rafael CA.  Pilot price $350.  Spaces are limited to 26.

Does your coaching niche involve working with "baby boomers"?

The first wave of the "baby boom" generation is just reaching their early 50's and receiving their invitations to join the American Association of Retired People (AARP).  This generation is redefining the image of growing older and the AARP magazine has been re-inventing itself to focus much more on the needs of this group. AARP magazine is working on a project involving coaching and topics of special interest to baby boomers.  This might include changing careers, getting back in the dating game, risk-taking, reviving long-lost goals, changing body image --- the possibilities are hugely varied. The project is still in it's early stages so the magazine is not yet sure what shape it may take.

They are looking for certified coaches who specialize in working with baby boomers or with issues that may be of special interest to baby boomers. If you are a CPCC interested in possibly participating in this project, send your contact and specialty information to kathy@thecoaches.com.  Please reference "AARP" in the subject line.
