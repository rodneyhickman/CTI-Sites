Coaching Business Tips

Submitted by members of the CTI community, these tips will help you move your coaching business forward successfully.

1. Know your success formula. “Every business has a formula toward getting clients. How many networking events will lead to how many sample sessions, which in turn will lead to how many clients. Use what works and modify it to include your own unique attraction. Example: when I was a one-person business, my formula was 3 speaking engagements (to groups of 10 or more) led to one bi-weekly teleclass of about 6-10 people on the class, which led to 1-3 personal coaching clients. Once you have the formula, just keep repeating it. It will save you time and money.”

— Mai Vu, CPCC

2. Accept credit cards. “My business really 'grew up' when I started accepting credit card payments. Before that, I accepted only checks from clients, and my clients would often mail their checks to me late, no matter how often I tried to redesign the alliance. While it made many days throughout the month feel like Christmas (oh look! a check in the mailbox!), it made it very hard to budget, make business investments, etc. Accepting payment through credit cards has allowed me to get paid fully at the first of the month from all my clients, letting my assistant and me decide what to do with my money rather than sit around waiting for it!

— Jeff Jacobson, CPCC
Jeff uses Practice Pay Solutions.
