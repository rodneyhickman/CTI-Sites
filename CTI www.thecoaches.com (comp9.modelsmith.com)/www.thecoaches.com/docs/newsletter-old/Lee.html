Leadership Through Passive Income

Have you ever wondered why passive income is so interesting to coaches? Is it because we’re lazy and want an easy buck, for minimal effort? Or maybe we love coaching, but want to stop doing so much of it and instead learn the ins and outs of the Internet instead of interacting with other human beings?

Not hardly! I think there’s an entirely different and sometimes subconscious reason.

Passive income is compelling to coaches because it gives us a way to deliver coaching to a much, much bigger segment of the population than we ever could solely one-on-one. It’s a different way to look at it, isn’t it? In fact, let me go just a little bit further. I believe that coaches who seek to lead in their work will ultimately ask themselves, “How can I contribute to lasting change of the greatest order, beyond myself?” 

One of those answers is by creating passive products of the highest quality that outlive you. Have you ever thought about writing an e-book? What’s an e-book after all, right? Isn’t it just a couple of written pages slapped together on a given topic with a pretty cover put on? Well, some people seem to make it magically put money in their bank accounts too…oh yes, to the tune of four and five figures each month. Now that’s passive income!

But the e-book I’d like to share with you is special for a different reason. It’s written by Tom Heck of Asheville, NC, coach and founder of TeachMeTeamwork.com, and is called Team Activities on a Shoestring: How to Use Two Normal Shoestrings to Lead 8 Fun and Engaging Team Building Activities. Here’s just one of hundreds of comments from Tom’s client, Don Clair, about the powerful coaching results the e-book and TeachMeTeamwork.com helped create:

“I had the opportunity to use your website as a resource for a learning activity a few days ago. I was working with teens that were considered to be challenging and viewed themselves as someone that would not fit into a team setting. I chose an activity that allows a participant to see how challenging life is without cooperation of those around them and how important communication is in every situation. The activity was fun, the kids learned how valuable teamwork is and that sometimes doing something alone is not practical. Thank you for making this resource available to the world and especially to teachers, treatment providers and counselors. Learning should always be fun and you have helped me make learning fun for some very challenging students and clients.”
—Don Clair,Program Development Specialist, Anthony Robbins Foundation,Retired Drug Treatment Counselor, Bureau of Prisons

These are words of high praise for any coach’s work, much less his e-book and website, yes?

Well, it goes on. This 18-page e-book is truly the little e-book that could. Tom receives emails from around the world from places as far away as remote Asia, South America and elsewhere, telling him of the change being created as a result of his coaching e-book.

In addition to the eight team exercises inside, it’s an example of how a coaching approach can be replicated in print. These include debriefing or processing suggestions, so that the teams get the most from each activity. They include guidance about setting boundaries, creating a safe place to build trust and alternatives for when fear occurs during the activities.

Does this give you some ideas for how a coach can create passive income in the form of a leading e-book? You can download your own copy at TeachMeTeamwork.com to see for yourself.

The last thing I’ll share about Tom’s e-book is this: It’s the most popular team-building games e-book ever written and has been downloaded more than 20,000 times since he wrote it, and it’s free.

If you don’t already have something, besides a sample session, that you give people at no cost—such as an e-book or report, audio interview or sample CD—I recommend you pursue it now. By giving so many people an opportunity to get to know, like and trust him through something that doesn’t require his time, Tom’s built a database of individuals who buy his multimedia CDs, books, assessments and even physical teambuilding games on a regular basis. And, of course, when the time comes, they ask him for one-on-one coaching, workshops and consulting, too.

For now, let me leave you with just a few thoughts.

You can create passive income many ways. Above is one leadership-based way to do it. An e-book was the central example of this article. There are countless other examples of spectacular audio programs, podcasts, written workbooks, CD and DVD sets created by coaches who are painting with that bigger brush and leaving a legacy beyond themselves. The topics of these passive income products vary as widely as how to help your child through your divorce or surviving the loss of a family member, to peaceful pregnancies and expressing your femininity. 

What they all have in common is:

  1. They are a solution to a problem experienced by a group of people who identify with each other and congregate or “hang out” together. (They’re easily accessible that way.)

  2. They are packaged in a way that is most valuable to the clients. (Are they aural, visual or kinesthetic learners? Package your passive products accordingly.)

  3. They are on the receiving end of a planned and sustained effort of people who will find it useful (and may not be readily open to coaching in the one-on-one fashion).

  4. They have a lead-in product that is no or low-cost that doesn’t require the coach’s time to deliver. (Hence, the word passive.) We call these lead-in products pink spoons.

There is a tremendous amount of information about passive income in the world. You might almost scratch your head at how “active” you have to be about acquiring it!

What works, after more than six years of proving, testing, tweaking and refining all sorts of strategies, tools and approaches, is the above. What doesn’t work is if you think passive income means easy money for little effort. What also doesn’t work is if you don’t have a bigger reason than money to sustain you in the learning curve.

If you want to leave a lasting legacy of your accumulated coaching wisdom and messages, consider passive income products. Whether you have five one-on-one clients or 50, you CAN make money and meaning in a sustainable way as a coach using a combined approach.
