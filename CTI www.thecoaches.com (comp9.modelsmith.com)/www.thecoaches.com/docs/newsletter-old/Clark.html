What’s Your Plan B?
Creating financial wellness with passive income

As coaches, we are often solely responsible for our financial well-being. Therefore, it’s critical that we be mindful of how to best leverage our time and resources. This has been a desire of mine for nearly six years but not something that I actively tended to until almost three years ago.

I’m a Co-Active coach and part of the CTI faculty. I live in Mexico a few blocks from the Caribbean Sea, and I’m living my dream. Since I coach and lead workshops for a living, the only way I make money at that is when I personally am talking to my clients or standing in front of a classroom. What that means is if illness, family emergencies or hurricanes (in my case) show up, the paychecks stop coming in. So how can we coaches best ensure our financial health? Passive income.

The idea here, of course, is to have your money work for you, so that you make money while you sleep!

I’m a huge fan of Robert T. Kiyosaki, author of the best selling book, Rich Dad, Poor Dad. I even participated in his financial coaching program to get training and support on how to create alternate income streams. So here’s what I’ve created:

First, I bought an old one-story home here in Mexico and converted it into two one-bedroom apartments, which I now rent out. I built a second story, which is where I live. The rental income from the two apartments pays for the mortgage, taxes and upkeep of the entire house. I live for free! My home is truly an asset because it is generating a positive cash flow. I also bought some land a few years ago that I hope to develop one day. Yes, the land has value and it’s appreciating, but technically it is not an asset because it is not generating income, so my land currently is a liability. Robert gave me a whole new way to look at my finances and it was a huge eye-opener. Consider reading his book titled The Cash Flow Quadrant.

Secondly, I joined a network marketing company this year. I know. I know what you must be thinking…pyramid scheme, those things never work, you can’t make money doing that, etc. Well, I tell you what—you might want to do a little research because the MLM industry has changed dramatically in the last 20 years. There are some excellent companies out there with strong, fair compensation structures that do work and can offer substantial financial rewards by working on a very part-time basis. The good ones will even let your residual income go to a family member or loved one. I don’t know how else we could make such a low initial investment of time and energy with the opportunity for such great financial rewards for a lifetime and beyond (even when we stop working the business!). You might also want to read Robert’s book The Business School, for People Who Like Helping People. It’s all about the network marketing industry and tells you what you need to know to make it work for you.

Now, does owning a rental income property and being part of a network marketing company require some time and attention? You bet…but not a lot. And the rewards are more than worth it. I now know that I have two income streams that can support me in case, for whatever reason, I no longer can or want to do my day job. I have a Plan B.

Robert Kiyosaki identifies six obstacles that must be overcome to achieve financial freedom. They are:

  1. fear
  2. cynicism
  3. laziness
  4. bad habits
  5. arrogance
  6. disappointment

If any of these resonate with you, work on them with your coach. Then begin to brainstorm your own Plan B for residual income. Are you drawn to investing? Real estate? Buying a business or a franchise? Or perhaps a lower startup cost venture such as a network marketing company? Maybe it’s time to write that bestseller that’s been on your brain. The key is to not let any of the six obstacles keep you from securing your financial future.

We do really important work as coaches. I know that we are making a contribution to mankind that is essential, inspiring and full of love. We deserve, as I believe everyone does, an abundant life that is not dictated by the size of our paycheck or what we charge for our services. What step will you take this week to ensure a lifetime of financial wellness?
