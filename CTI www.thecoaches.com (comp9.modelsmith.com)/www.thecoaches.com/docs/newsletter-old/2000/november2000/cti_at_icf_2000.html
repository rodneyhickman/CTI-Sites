Staff, students and leaders of The Coaches Training Institute, along with a thousand or so others, descended on the Westin-Bayshore Hotel in Vancouver, Canada for the 5th Annual International Coach Federation Conference from October 26th - 28th. The CTI booth was a hub of activity almost from the moment it was constructed. People who hadn't seen one another since they'd sat in their first course reconnected, and people who'd never met (except on JumpStart or Certification pod calls) put faces to voices. The energy was amazing. (The chocolates we were giving out at the booth, with wrappers that sported our new logo, probably added to that energy!)

On Friday night, CTI held a reception in the Westin's beautiful Seawall Bistro; good food, good drink and good company! Two of CTI's founders, Karen Kimsey-House and Laura Whitworth gathered the crowd to introduce staff members, and to tell them about their recent visit to jail (see story CTI Goes to Jail). After hearing the story of their experiences with introducing inmates to the power and possibility of Co-Active Coaching, there wasn't a dry eye in the house. The work that we do really DOES have the power to change lives.

We'll be back next year at the ICF Conference in Chicago...hope we see you there!
