Co-Active Coaching
New Skills for Coaching People Toward
Success in Work and Life

Laura Whitworth, Henry Kimsey-House and Phil Sandahl
Foreword by John Whitmore, author of Coaching for Performance

oaching as a profession is
experiencing dramatic growth. From senior executives at Fortune 100 companies, to the self-employed, to people in transition, people at the point of change are looking more and more to coaches for guidance and support. For professional coaches who want to increase their proficiency as well as for those interested in integrating coaching skills into their consulting practice, Co-Active Coaching presents a revolutionary new collaborative approach. Developed by three pioneers and leading authorities in the coaching field, this approach allows the client and coach to work together to identify both work-related and personal areas of development.
The authors espouse a unique new model of coaching --- co-active coaching. This model encompasses five specific skills sets and the three key principles of coaching: fulfillment, balance and process," Fulfillment is about who we choose to be, balance is about how we shape our lives, process is about how we move through the world," say the authors.

Co-Active Coaching offers a comprehensive view of the practice of coaching and features instructive coaching examples, skill-building exercises for practitioners to use with clients and coaching tips and traps.
LAURA WHITWORTH, cofounder of Coaches Training Institute, was named one of the ten most influential coaches in 1996 by Professional Coach Journal. HENRY KIMSEY-HOUSE is cofounder of the Coaches Training Institute and Curriculum Director of the Co-Active Leadership Program. PHIL SANDAHL is cofounder of the Minnesota Coaches Association.
