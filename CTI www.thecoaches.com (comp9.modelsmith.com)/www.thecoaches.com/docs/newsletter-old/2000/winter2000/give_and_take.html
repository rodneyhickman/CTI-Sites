Smokin Smokie gets Certified!
Smokie Sizemore & Company has been certified as a Women's Business Enterprise Company in Richmond, VA, which effectively puts the company on the map for procuring government contracts. The program is sponsored by the Virginia Small Business Development Center. Virginia governmental agencies are required to do at least five percent of their business with women-owned companies. Private corps. set similar goals. Congrats, Smokie. Go get'em, girl!

They Have a Dream
On December 13th, 1998, Judith Wilson and her partner, Jeff Jacobson, took their dream into action by conducting the first meeting of the Worldwide Alliance of Black Coaches, thus giving a unified voice to coaches of African heritage. They also released the first in a series of training's entitled, "Coaching for Effective Alliance Building A Way to Coach Across Diverse Lines." Contact Judith and Jeff at 510-655-3743.

RENEWAL for Women with ADD
Hope Langner, MS, a coach from Albany, NY, and Sari Solden, MFCC, author of the book, Women with Attention Deficit Disorder have created RENEWAL, a program designed especially for women with ADD. The first RENEWAL overnight getaway was held this past June. The focus was on relaxation, connection, and learning, and introduced coaching to 30 women. RENEWAL retreats will continue in 1999. Contact Hope at: Hopina@aol.com.

am often struck by the loyalty of those who love us. Why, I wonder, in this crazy world, do people take the time to share with a friend, to email me a piece of feedback about something that isn't working, or to come and assist at the back of the room? I so appreciate this level of relationship, of participation. Clearly these people gain something themselves from giving. Still, I am awestruck by their generosity.

It's nothing new to say that CTI is a volunteer based organization. Much of what we have accomplished has been a result of support from out extended community. As we be in community together, organized around our commitment to coaching, we nourish and enrich each other. I do know that there is something special about the energy of CTI.

At the heart of that difference, I feel, are the fundamental principles of Co-active Coaching; that people are naturally creative, resourceful and whole, as well as the fact that we place a high value on collaborative, designed alliances with those around us.

Also, I know that we draw to us and have in our community a very unique and special caliber of people; people who want to make a difference and who have a larger vision for what is possible in the world.

And so, beginning with the new year, we will be implementing a CTI Volunteer Program. In San Rafael, we are setting up a special
