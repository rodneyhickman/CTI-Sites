On the Co-active Network, one of our goals is to keep the Main Community discussion area focused on collegial conversation and open discussion of concepts hoping to encourage coaches to share examples, experiences, resources, or references in an ongoing dialog. Because we realize that member coaches have products and services to promote, we have created structures to accommodate and categorize those promotions. These structures appear as folders at the top of the Main Community Discussion Area.

Any posting related to products, services, classes, teleconferences, or business development (not an exhaustive list) is considered a promotion and should be placed in the appropriate promotions folder. Inside each category folder is a description of content appropriate for that space. Follow the links below to learn more about each category:

Client/Coach Match
Promotions & Announcements

      Calls, Events, Training, Conferences

      Free Resource Sharing

      Professional Services Offered and Requested

      $$$$ Products: Books, CDs, etc.

In general, please refrain from posting the same material more than once per week. One of the reasons we request restraint in promotional posting is the impact multiple posting has on people receiving posts as real time emails as well as the impact inside the Daily Digest. Multiple identical posts create a "stacking" effect in the in-boxes and Daily Digests of your fellow Network members. Think about it: this is behavior we see from spammers and can alienate the very people you are trying to reach. Assess your impact before you post.

Please review the Co-active Network Posting Protocol for more details on publishing promotional material.

Reviewing promotional material posted by other Network members is a great way to generate new ideas for your own marketing efforts. These posts provide a wealth of successful and not-so-successful approaches to product and services positioning as well as crafting your marketing messages.

Let me know if there is something on the Co-Active Network you’d like to learn more about.

Over the past several years, CTI has fielded a surprising volume of inquiry about localized training from around the world, especially growing and developing nations such as India and China. CTI already offers Co-active training publicly in more than a dozen countries and is among the longest standing coach training firms in existence.

CTI management recognized that the organization could follow one of two possible paths. We could imitate most other American training companies expanding into additional countries and fly instructors in from the United States to teach a course in English a few times per year, make some money and then leave; or stay true to the essence of what we do which is to create community and more specifically a community of coaches. Ultimately CTI wasn’t willing to move into markets where we weren’t able to make a commitment to build and cultivate a community over time.

The challenge was to find a way to accomplish this vision feasibly, economically, and logistically. Then came the realization that CTI needed help and that we needed a partner to lean into to make this happen. CTI began working with an established training company in the Netherlands, Schouten & Nelissen, to create a new co-owned business venture to realize the vision of how Co-active coaching could unfold around the world.

Schouten & Nelissen engages in different work than CTI, but at its heart is in alignment with the Co-active model by helping people manifest lives of choice and move beyond limiting patterns of belief. Together we have created a joint venture called Coactive Partners with Paul Byrne serving as Managing Director. Schouten Training, the international division of S&N has 50% ownership, CTI has 50% ownership. Co-active Partners’ job is to take Co-active coaching and leadership into the developing world, particularly Asia and Eastern Europe.

This is a co-active relationship with both organizations leaning into the other to create something profoundly different than either could do alone. We have CTI’s content, intellectual property and Co-active brand of coaching and Schouten Training’s international distribution network, partners, and local offices. Combining these assets gives us the most widely recognized
brand of coaching in the world powered by an infrastructure that effectively doubles the population that can be reached. Suddenly, 3 billion more people can access Co-active work which will be both globally recognizable and locally unique.

The business structures we are creating will be localized and the long-term goal is to have the course leader structure localized like we already see in Europe, with a community of leaders working in their markets, leading in their language and cultural context. Local wisdom will then in turn be incorporated into the Co-active model. The days in which information flows one way are going to be relatively short. As ideas are exchanged, we expect other cultures will be teaching us a lot about the work that we do.

Co-active Partners is taking a regional and then a local strategy. For example, there is a strategy to grow Co-active coaching in China over the next 5 years. Training started in Beijing this year and will expand into Shanghai in 2011. Our local partners, Shouten China, are working on plans to be in five additional cities in 2012. CTI will be supporting them and following their lead. Co-active Partners will not just be serving the large international coastal cities, but will move into major population hubs in the southwest and northwest, so that we begin serving all of China. Similar plans will be developed for India, Russia, Eastern Europe, and beyond.

What does this mean for you? It makes you part of the fastest growing, most vibrant community of coaches worldwide.
