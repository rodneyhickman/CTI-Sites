Letter from the President

The morning sun is as warm and golden as honey as it pours through my office window. I love Fall. It feels as if the earth is winding down, settling from all the bursting forth of Spring and the rich harvest of late Summer. I find myself in deep appreciation of the constancy of change and filled with gratitude for the rich tapestry of my life.

When I am able to relax into the state or condition rather than just listing the things for which I am grateful, gratitude is the most healing thing I know.

In this state of gratitude, I find that I am able to let go of my worrying mind and of the judge within that evaluates things as good or bad. Whatever comes is a gift to be embraced and savored. Quite separate from any circumstances, there is ease, flow and true freedom.

Of course I am not able to reside permanently in this state of gratitude. It requires a consistent practice of finding myself captured in reaction and/or judgment to “recover” to this state of gratitude. The recovery can happen in an instant. Often it takes hours and sometimes days. As I practice, I find that little by little, the times of reaction are growing shorter and the state of gratitude longer. Hooray!

I am not a master of this practice, not by any stretch of the imagination. And yet I sense that the practicing is enough. It is the intention, rather than the perfection of this practice that is so incredibly healing.

In fact, the more challenging the circumstances, the more I fail and recover, the stronger my intention becomes. Thus it is the failure and falling away (and the ensuing recovery) that strengthens me, not an illusion of perfection.

This is my wish for the world. . .this practice of recovering to a state of gratitude. I know this isn’t a particularly new or novel concept and yet I sense that it is important. With each passing year, the challenges facing human kind grow more complex. What if the key lay, not only in what we did, in the brilliant solutions that we were able to discover together, but also in our state of being while we were doing it? In this way, perhaps the simple practice of recovering to the state gratitude could shift everything.


Love,
Karen

Karen Kimsey-House
President

Managing Daily Digests

If you are active in several communities on the Co-active Network, you may find yourself on the receiving end of many daily digests. Did you know you can turn off the daily emails from individual communities?

Login to http://www.coactivenetwork.com then click on the "Manage Account" link near the top of the green box at the left edge of screen. Here you will find a list of communities you are joined to under the heading "My Communities." You will see two columns of boxes, one labeled "Daily Digest," and the other labeled "Real-time Notifications."

If you do not want any email from a community, uncheck both boxes to cease email delivery for that community. If you want a summary of each day's activities sent to you once per day, check the box for "Daily Digest." (This is the default when you join any community.) If you want to receive an email as soon as somebody posts or responds, check the box for "Real-time Notifications." Finish by clicking the "Save Changes" button.

If you are traveling and want to temporarily halt all daily digest emails from the Co-active Network, check the "Suspend Mail" box on the "Manage Account" screen. This will stop all email from every community you are joined to until you return and remove the check in the box. Don't forget to click the "Save Changes" button to make your changes stick.
