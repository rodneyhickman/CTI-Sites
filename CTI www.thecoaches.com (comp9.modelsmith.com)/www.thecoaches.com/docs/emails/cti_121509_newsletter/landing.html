Letter from the President

What a year 2009 has been! Profound. Intense. Challenging. Transformational. One of the things I am noticing recently is how much closer and more connected people feel. Challenge has a way of doing that—of reminding us who we truly are and what is most important.

So as you move into what can sometimes be a frenzied time, remember to keep it simple and light. People truly are more important to your life than things. Our greatest possession is resonance of our own true heart and the greatest gift you can give to anyone is the gift of your listening, attention and love.

Thank you for your belief in the Co-active Way and for being a part of the CTI family. From our “house” to yours, best wishes for a magical holiday season filled with authentic, intimate connection and the joy of belonging.


In Gratitute,
Karen

Karen Kimsey-House
President

New Year! New You?

The impending New Year is a great time to take a fresh look at things, which makes this the perfect time to update your Co-Active Network member profile. Update your progress through the CTI curriculum. Have you accomplished anything recently that would impress? Tell us in your profile. New business name or partner? Get the word out. Oh, and ask someone to take your photo so that you can add your fantastic face to your profile. Come on! You know you want to!

It is very easy to edit your profile. Simply go to http://www.coactivenetwork.com/ and log in using your email address and password. Then click the “Edit Profile” link near the top of the green box at the left edge of the screen. Make any changes you need then click the “Save Profile” button. Don’t forget that last step because if you don’t click the “Save Profile” button, your changes will be lost.

Please let me know if there is something you’d like to learn how to do on the Co-active Network.


Warmly,
Marcia Norris, Co-active Network Liaison

Upcoming Main Community Events

LEADERSHIP TEAMS AND CO-ACTIVE COACHING:
PLAYING TO WIN RATHER THAN PLAYING NOT TO LOSE
Wednesday, February 3, 2010 at Noon PT/3:00 pm ET
 What happens when organizational leaders and their teams shift from a “play not to lose” approach to a “playing to win” perspective? If all organizational energy is currently spent trying to avoid unwanted negative outcomes, how can a team possibly aspire to achieve meaningful vision? What role can co-active coaching play in the process of shifting a team’s stance?

In this conversation, John Vercelli and Art Shirk will facilitate discussion on topics such as:

  • Research from the field that links adult development to how leadership teams become most effective.
  • How co-active coaching transforms leadership perspectives and supports transformative development.
  • Distinctions between reactive (e.g. saboteur-driven) ways of responding versus creative (e.g. empowered and resonant) strategies for leaders and leadership teams.
