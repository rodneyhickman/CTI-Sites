TLC Certification

TLC Certification Impacts Coaches Personally and Professionally

For the past year, CTI has been offering The Leadership Circle Profile (TLCP) Certification Training to coaches who have completed core curriculum. Part of the certification process is individually completing the profile process and experiencing the subsequent interpretation. Not surprisingly, the impact of the TLCP training process on our students is both personal and professional.

Jeremy Stover described his experience with TLCP as “the most powerful instrument I’ve ever experienced. Its ability to surface and clearly identify key areas for growth and development even among people who are quite self-aware and have done a great deal of work on themselves is truly astounding. It is like doing a deep dive into oneself for the sake of better understanding and working with one’s strengths and weaknesses.”

“The day I got back from the training, I had to deal with a major issue,” explains Joss Rowlands about her work environment. “Listening to myself talk to a number of people on the phone gave me some insights into what I sounded like and how I handled myself and them. As a result, I’ve totally changed a couple of approaches I used to take when needing to guide others over whom I had no direct responsibility.”

Leslie Clark acknowledges that The Leadership Circle Training has benefitted her as a coach. “My coaching is far more masterful with all of my clients now even if they have not taken TLCP. The training gave me a whole new way to listen to my clients. I quickly identify their reactive tendencies and therefore am now able to bust their saboteurs more readily.”

Stover finds that offering the assessment builds his credibility as a coach. “Because of the theory and foundation of the model, it establishes that the coach knows what he or she is talking about and that there is something here for the client to learn about that is not fluff or the standard HR professional development mumbo jumbo.”

“I’m finding that during this economic downturn, companies are far more receptive to hiring me to administer a tool that includes coaching as opposed to just coaching,” continues Clark. “They want something tangible—they really appreciate the unique qualities TLCP has to offer…it’s not just another 360. Using TLCP provides a broader view for the client that allows us to go deeper and do more impactful work.”

If you are interested in generating personal and professional impacts like these in your own coaching practice, consider attending TLC Profile Certification Training in San Rafael, CA, on October 26-28, 2010. Deadline for registration is October 11, 2010.

Fundamentals Course in China

Honoring the First Fundamentals Course Taught in China

As a summer rain chased away the dryness and heat from Beijing, China on July 8, 2010, the first Fundamentals course offered publicly in China began. CTI Senior Faculty members Jeff Jacobson and Lauren Powers led a room full of professional Chinese nationals working at major consulting companies and multinational corporations.

“While it‘s exciting to be able to bring Co-active Coaching to a new country, it’s always a little scary too,” admits Karen Kimsey-House. “There‘s always a slight hesitation. Will the model communicate? Will people from such a different culture be able to relate to what we are trying to say?

“Yet in my heart, I always know it will be ok,” Karen asserts. “There is something about the model of Co-Active Coaching that invites people to meet beyond our differences in a place that is very real and very human. When it comes right down to it, we are not so different after all.”

As Jeff and Lauren set-up the first exercise, observer Paul Byrne noted that folks looked a bit confused, hesitant and unsure of the CTI style of training. “I must admit I was a little worried about how the design and our concepts would play in this culture.” But as the attendees got up from their chairs, Paul’s fears were put to rest. As he listened to the dialog of the pair directly in front of him, Paul relaxed and thought to himself, “you are so in the right room!”

Later, Paul overheard one of the participants say he thought the secret to great coaching was to find your true self, and have that person see the true self in another - and then just dance. “I think he's on the right track...” Yes Paul, we have to agree with you.

More Co-active courses are scheduled in China during the fall. You can find a list (in Chinese) on our partner website: http://www.schoutenchina.com/.
