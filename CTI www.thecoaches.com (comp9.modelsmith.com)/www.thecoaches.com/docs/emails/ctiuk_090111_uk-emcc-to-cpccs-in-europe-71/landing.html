About 18 months ago, CTI became aware of the growing reputation of the European Mentoring and Coaching Council (EMCC). It was clear that while the ICF was the best established organisation globally; the EMCC, with more than 3,000 members in over 30 countries, represents a significant entry point into a European community of coaches and mentors sharing similarly high ethical values and professional skills.

In our desire to continue to ensure our courses are widely recognised for the high standards we set for both our trainers and participants, CTI undertook the process to become EMCC accredited.

We are delighted to announce that CTI has successfully been awarded the EMCC’s European Quality Award (EQA) at the following levels:

  • Foundation level for our Fundamentals course, and
  • Practitioner level for a combination of our Intermediate (Core Curriculum), Certification, and Leadership Circle Profile courses

So how does this affect you?

That depends on whether you choose to pursue individual accreditation through the EMCC’s European Individual Award (EIA), or not. If you do, the pathway will be considerably easier than if CTI was not an EMCC accredited training provider.

Ultimately, whether you pursue an EMCC accreditation or not, is down to you, and how you choose to position and market yourself as a coach. Our concern is that you have a choice. To help you better understand if this is the right choice for you, please read the FAQs we have prepared on the topic.

While we can field questions about how our courses, including the Leadership Circle certification, relate to becoming accredited by the EMCC, we cannot offer counsel or advice about the actual process of individual accreditation by the EMCC.

Wishing you every success in your continuing professional development as a coach.
