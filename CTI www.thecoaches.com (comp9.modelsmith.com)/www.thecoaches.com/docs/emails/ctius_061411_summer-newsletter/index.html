Crossing Boundaries in Singapore

By Poorni Bid

On June 3, 2011 CTI expanded its global footprint with the launch of the first full Co-Active Coach training series in Singapore. Faculty members Abi Shilon and Emma Wheat led a Fundamentals course for a group of participants whose professional backgrounds and ethnic and national origins reflected perfectly the cosmopolitan nature of life in Singapore. Participants included a number of human resources professionals from Cargill working in the Asia-Pacific region, a high school physics teacher, a writer, an international civil servant and a number of current and retired Singaporean civil servants.

Abi, an Israeli, and Emma, a Brit living in Dubai, led a participant group which included Singaporeans of both Chinese and Indian descent, several Chinese nationals, a Philippine-American, two more Brits, a Frenchman, and an Australian. What an incredible “first moment” in the world of Co-Active Coaching. The group came hungry to learn and willing to play, from the very first mingle right through to completion. The course was another beautiful instance of how Co-Active Coaching reaches across national, ethnic and professional boundaries, allowing individuals to connect first and foremost as human beings.

All of these individuals will continue their journey through the summer, completing Fulfillment, Balance and Process together and creating a beachhead for Co-Active Coaching in South East Asia.

By Sabrina Roblin

CTI continues to grow internationally and in response to this growth, CTI recently held Front of Room Leader auditions in the Netherlands for specific language needs in our training courses. These languages included Norwegian, Spanish, German and Dutch. It is always a treat to audition leaders – to meet, get to know and work with the graduates of our programs in this way. As we get to know each audition participant, this one day experience stretches their skills and understanding of the being and doing of Co-Active Leadership. Everyone learns a lot in the process regardless of the outcome of the experience.

As you read this you may wonder how the process of auditioning and choosing a leader works. Whenever we open applications for auditions, we send out an email to our database of people who have completed both the Co-Active Leadership program and have their Certification Professional Co-Active Coach certification. The application includes a recorded coaching session along with a self-critique of the coaching. After reviewing all applications, we choose the people we want to audition, then at the audition we select the people who we will train to become our next Front of Room Leaders.

The next wave of auditions will likely be in China as we roll-out courses there. The prerequisites to becoming a CTI course leader are on the CTI web site.

By Marla Skibbins

"Do not dwell in the past, do not dream of the future,
concentrate the mind on the present moment."
- The Buddha

I believe that one of the most important ways coaches can serve their clients is to guide and support them in being present in this moment, whatever the moment brings. Sometimes it’s hard for clients to stay with the present moment. They might be in grief about a difficulty or in elation about a victory. Sometimes it is delightful to be in this moment and sometimes it isn’t. Most of the time it is a mixture; like when a client is both scared and empowered to make a impactful choice that will bring about change.

Clients may wonder why coaches focus on being present. But think about it; the present moment is all that any of us have. Our whole life is a string of moment-to-moment experiences of what is happening right now. Everything else: all the conversations in our heads, all our ideas, our concerns, our thoughts about the future or the past, our worries and strivings, take us away from a deeper truth that comes when we are simply attentive to this present moment. The insight and truths that arise from being present may be about deeper understandings of reality or they may be about what to do next in our career, what is needed in our relationship or how to balance our finances.

Most of us have been taught that the way to resolve our difficulties is to analyze them and think about them. We don’t have practice simply letting our wisdom arise from presence, and letting this deeper wisdom inform us on what to do next. Over and over, with my clients and in my own life, I see how just being present brings insights that resolve situations. When clients get present, new solutions come that were not available just through thinking and analyzing. Decisions get made and the next appropriate actions become clear just from being present and in this moment.

When we are mindful of the present moment we can access our complete experience: the rise and fall of a breath in our chest, the wind on our face, the sense of courage in our heart and the energy of aliveness in our body. We can fully experience the embrace of a child, the exquisite taste of that first sip of coffee in the morning and the awe of the deeper mystery of reality.

These experiences bring joy and completeness to our lives. These moments have us feel like we are IN our lives not just thinking about them. That is what we want for ourselves and that is what we want for our clients.
