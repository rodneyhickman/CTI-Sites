Co-Active Coaching is a formal relationship strategy intended to support the client in meeting their developmental needs and goals within the organization. The internal coach toolkit provides internal coaches with tools that enable them to effectively use the Co-Active coaching model and skills with internal clients to increase employee engagement, fulfillment and productivity. The tools and resources are organized to coincide with four major stages in the coaching relationship.

The tools and resources are organized in four main areas:

Selection

These tools are designed to support the organization in the process of identifying individuals for coaching, determining their readiness and matching them to an appropriate coach.
more »

Purpose:

  • Identify the right individuals for coaching
  • Ensure “coaching readiness”
  • Match coach/client

Success Criteria:

  • Alignment to business and individual goals
  • Coach-client partnership has high probability of success

Process:

  • Identify client via referral, request, or problem escalation
  • Assess readiness
  • Identify internal or external coach

Start-up & Assessment

This section includes tools to help understand the business context, assess the developmental needs of the individual and design the alliance between the coach, the client and the organizational stake holders.
more »

Purpose:

  • Understand business context
  • Design the alliance between coach, client, and other stakeholders
  • Assess developmental needs

Success Criteria:

  • Coach credibility and trust
  • Clarity of coaching alliance
  • Assessment data as basis for coaching

Process:

  • Gather and review organizational information
  • Design coaching alliance with client
  • Conduct assessment(s)
  • Use Co-Active Coaching with assessment data

Implementation

These tools support the longest stage of the coaching relationship which is about ongoing learning and performance improvement. They will help you navigate ethical dilemmas, clarify your clients purpose, values and vision while holding them accountable for their goals and action plans.
more »

Purpose:

  • Ongoing learning and performance improvement through coaching
  • Recognize and navigate ethical dilemmas and legal issues
  • Clarify purpose, vision, and values
  • Establish clear goals and action plans

Success Criteria:

  • Clear statements of purpose, vision, and values
  • Specific goals, action plans, and accountabilities
  • Sound ethical decision-making

Process:

  • Conduct regular ongoing coaching sessions
  • Create development plan
  • Use tools to foster purpose, vision, and values
  • Use decision making process to navigate ethical dilemmas

Measurement & Closure

These tools are to create a measurement strategy to assess the effectiveness of the internal coaching process. These tools also support internal coaches in bringing coaching engagements to completion with clarity and intention.
more »

Purpose:

  • Bring coaching relationship to close
  • Establish continuation of development
  • Plan measurement strategy and tools for internal coaching engagement
  • Measure progress
  • Measure coach’s effectiveness

Success Criteria:

  • Coaching process viewed as successful by client and coach
  • Ongoing learning and performance improvement at individual and group level
  • Improved learning and performance metrics
  • Positive coaching effectiveness metrics
  • Effective measurement plan

Process:

  • Debrief coaching process and outcomes
  • Conduct final client assessment
  • Coaching Effectiveness Evaluation tool
  • Revise development plan
  • Conduct final coach assessment
