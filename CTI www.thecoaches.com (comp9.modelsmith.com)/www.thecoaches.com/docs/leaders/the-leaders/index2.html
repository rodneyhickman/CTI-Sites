Coach Training Faculty

Tony Barton, CPCC, PCC

Tony is Mr Red Kite – or at least that’s how many people know him through his coaching business ‘Red Kite World‘. After a successful 20 year career in marketing, Tony left his ‘velvet rut’ and discovered some really interesting products to market through coaching – like aliveness, joy and courage. He is an authorized Team Diagnostic Facilitator and an MBTI assessor. Tony brings many ‘red kite’ qualities to his work as a coach and front of room leader and is known for his great sense of play, fun and groundedness. In addition to his work with CTI he also runs his own ‘Simply Inspiring’ workshops and adventure holidays. Married with 3 grown children, Tony lives in the UK.

Dori Ben-Chanoch, PCC, CPCC

Dori specializes in Executive Coaching, working closely in one-on-one settings with executives in order to hone their skills. Dori is a certified behavioral coach, who has worked in training and development for the last 15 years. He specializes in executive presence, interpersonal communication skills, and emotional intelligence using a model of open and honest communication. In the past, Dori served as a president of a successful financial services company, and has extensive experience as a top sales producer and sales manager.

Ann Betz, CPCC

Ann has a passionate commitment to people living lives beyond their wildest dreams. She believes that this is not only possible, it is absolutely crucial in creating the kind of world we all want to live in. The wandering road of Ann's own life includes extensive work on leadership development with young leaders engaged in community service, as well as many years of training in both corporations and non-profit organizations. Since 2005, she has lived on a 55-acre farm in Costa Rica with her family, where she coaches, works with women in the local community, and commutes to the United States to lead workshops on spiritual development.

Eileen Blumenthal, JD, MA, CPCC, PCC

JD, Hastings College of Law
MA, Education, Harvard University
Eileen founded Rocket Science Coaching & Consulting in 1995 and works at the intersection of humor, insight and ease. She comes to coaching after careers in non-profit management, employment law, and higher education administration, always favoring work with individuals finding their way. In addition to serving on CTI’s faculty as an examiner and supervisor, Eileen provides coaching and consulting internationally, most recently with PEPFAR-funded agencies in Mozambique combating HIV/AIDS. She is the co-designer of, and lead trainer for, the Great Plains Public Health Leadership Institute peer coaching program for emerging public health leaders. Eileen is a Bikram yoga enthusiast, and she and her partner are the humble guardians of a very smart cattle dog, Gracie.

Sandra Cain, CPCC, PCC

Sandra coaches individuals, pairs and teams. Her background includes 15 years experience in large organizations with a variety of leadership and personal development roles. Her stand for this work is that while we are here, we might as well be conscious and intentional about who we are, what we do and how we live. Sandra also has a strong belief that life is meant to be a fun adventure and that just about everything is funny, given enough time. When not coaching or training, she loves rollerblading, hot yoga and cruising in a vintage speedboat with her husband and their beagles.

Veronica (Ronnie) Clifford, CPCC

Ronnie spent 20 years working in sales, marketing and commercial roles and 10 years working as a software engineer. She coaches corporate executives and coaches in training and a variety people in between. Ronnie delivers leadership training and personal development coaching for IBM UK, working at all levels within the organization to maximize the human potential that is available. Ronnie splits her time in the UK between London and Devon. She is a keen traveler and loves to work with people. Ronnie is never happier than when she is with her close friends & family or singing in the Choir!

Deborah Colman, MA, CPCC, MCC

MA, Education, University of Toronto, Workplace Learning and Change
Deborah loves to laugh and believes that when we cultivate joy in our lives we are better equipped to embrace the darkness. She believes that powerful learning occurs when people are having fun and values play as a powerful medium for generating learning that sticks. Deborah is passionate about humanizing the organizations in which we work and she inspires executives and high potential managers to think in possibility and embrace and excel in purposeful leadership. Prior to becoming a coach and trainer she worked in healthcare as a physical therapist and organizational development consultant. In her down time, Deborah enjoys the rhythmical life-giving movements of walking, conversation, wilderness canoe tripping and playing her guitar.

Cynthia Loy Darst, MFA, CPCC, MCC

Master of Fine Arts, University of California, San Diego
As one of the founders of the International Coach Federation (ICF) and one of the first to gain the designation of Master Certified Coach (MCC), Cynthia is internationally known as a pioneer in the world of coaching. She was one of the first leaders for The Coaches Training Institute. Cynthia also serves as the Director of Leader Development for The Center for Right Relationship (CRR). Passionate about raising the bar on the coaching profession, Cynthia has been developing new ideas for advanced coaches who are ready to take their work towards artistry and mastery. Playful and provocative, a day with Cynthia is guaranteed to bring new juice and fun to your coaching.

Grace Flannery, MS, CPCC, PCC

MS, Chemistry, University of Illinois
Grace is an executive and team coach, trainer and facilitator dedicated to the cultivation of exceptional leadership for individuals, teams and organizations. She is the founder and president of Leading Spirit, Inc., providing strategic services for leaders of non profit organizations since 1999. Grace applies her expertise in organizational development, relationship dynamics and change management to build clients’ capacity for skillful execution of their leadership agenda. She works with senior executives to ensure that their actions are grounded in wisdom and powered by purpose. Grace has 25 years experience in coaching, management and board positions in philanthropy, the arts, education, health and social services.

Kim Fowler, CPCC

Kim is Principal of MESA Coaching. She ignites individuals, executives and teams into mindful, efficient, spirited action. Kim combines her inspiration from the natural beauty of the Southwest to lead her clients to discard limitation and create professional and personal paths of growth, challenge, acceptance and joy. She has twenty-five years of management experience in the government, nonprofit and academic sectors. Kim also coaches individuals and family systems that have loved ones living with dementia. She focuses on opening new ways of communicating with themselves and the person(s) they have known for so long, whom it seems they now don’t know at all.

Caroline MacNeill Hall, MA, CPCC, PCC

Master of Arts, Human Development, St. Mary's University
Caroline is a senior CTI faculty member, executive coach and author who works with individuals and organizations to build leadership grounded in respect and fascination, authenticity and authority. A sunny soul who believes that laughter lubricates leading, Caroline is as elegant and fierce as she is funny and friendly. Caroline is all about the joy; it’s her covert agenda for client organizations. Before becoming a coach, she was a journalist and ghost author of 13 books, mostly for psychologists. Along with CTI co-founder Laura Whitworth and colleague Rick Tamlyn, Caroline is co-author of the book The Bigger Game.

Dorcas Kelley, MBA, CPCC, PCC, CMC

Master of Business Administration, University of California, Los Angeles
Dorcas has a driving passion to transform the world of coaching, one coach at a time, by helping each one discover their power and confidence as a leader in their coaching work, business and life. She balances a grounded presence with a wry sense of humor, playfully illuminating both our greatness and our humanity. In her roles of author, coach, trainer and consultant, she works with executives, business owners and entrepreneurs to reach the next level of success in their professional and personal lives. In addition, her book The Business of Coaching® is a popular resource for coaches, now in its 4th edition. When she’s not consumed by her work or her writing, you’ll find her hiking with her dogs, digging in her garden, or volunteering at local non-profits.

Roxane Loiseaux, MA, CPCC

MA, Philosophy, University of Amiens
Roxane works with senior executives in multinational corporations, directors of small companies as well as on systems within organizations, both as a trainer and as a coach. She brings her focus and rigor, together with warmth and lightness, at the service of individuals’ self-awareness, empowered choices and full presence. She supports individuals in aligning their lives by integrating the personal and the professional. Her holistic approach incorporates her philosophical background and her belief in the wisdom of the body. Roxane’s intent is for individuals to invest in themselves and in their lives, and by doing so, to contribute to the various systems they belong to—families, communities, organizations—to co-create a more sustainable future now.
