Co-Active Coaching in Germany

by Ute Kueffner, CPCC, ACC, and Hannes Entz von Zerssen, CPCC, PCC

It is from the place of fulfillment that we write this note to you all. It is June and the soccer world cup is taking place in Germany. The German team has played well and will move on to the next round. Juergen Klinsmann, the German coach, has brought on a good team spirit and changed the way of interacting with the players. It appears that he was able to install a sort of Future Self with the team. They have created a vision of themselves as a team. With this in mind it is almost secondary to win the game. It appears to be a win to play as well as possible in the moment. This permission is seemingly pulling them together as a team, and what is more surprising is that it is inspiring the whole country. We have never seen our country caught up in such a positive, inspired and fulfilled state since the fall of the Berlin wall in 1989. Fulfillment is happening in Germany.

Why are we writing all this right now? We are sitting in the first Fulfillment course ever held in Germany. It seems apt to write about the fulfillment of a dream we have had for some years. It was about bringing Co-Active Coaching to Germany. It was about bringing light to a place that had almost forgotten that life can be fun and exciting. 

We set out in August 2005 to establish Co-Active Coaching in Germany. Our major challenge was, and to a certain extent still is, twofold:

  • Germany is, in regard to coaching, a very established and mature market with a large number of varied coach training programs.
  • Co-Active coach training is only available in English and Japanese at this moment.  

In spite of this, we had our first Fundamentals course in Heidelberg in May 2006 and now in June our first Fulfillment course. The courses have been a huge success. The participants are a delight. They integrate the Co-Active Coaching model very naturally and with a lot of fun and ease. Most of the participants signed up for all five courses. We are in the process of organizing the next series, which will start in September 2006.

We write this note with huge gratitude to the participants, the Co-Active community, the CTI faculty and the founders Laura, Henry and Karen for inspiring us and keeping the dream alive. We have thrown a pebble into the water, and we are in awe of the ripples it has already created. What we see is that the Co-Active Coaching model works across cultures—it is universal.

Co-Active Coaching is doing well.

With love from Germany,
Hannes and Ute
