You can see that our Retirement Lifestyle Coaching community is exploring the new phenomenon of how retirement is changing—and changing fast, with more than 10,000 Baby Boomers turning 60 every day! Our community members are finding ways to partner with other professionals, such as financial planners, accountants, estate lawyers and health care professionals to provide the lifestyle component for these many retirees as they plan for active and successful retirement years.

Because the needs of the Baby Boomer generation are as diverse and elusive as they are enormous, our members are constantly searching for effective ways to market to this group. To assist in our learning and planning process, we recently conducted an online survey on what constitutes quality of life in retirement.

Respondents represented those 50 to 70 years old, mostly U.S. residents (80%, as compared to 20% Canadian residents). Only 12% are fully retired with 25% in semi-retirement and 22% planning to retire. Others are actively planning career changes, starting a new business or working full-time.

When asked to respond to the question, “For you, what constitutes quality of life in retirement?” they replied as follows:

  • Good health—90%
  • Financial well being—85%
  • Living your dreams—83%
  • Travel—76%
  • The opportunity to work—61%.

This list of priorities provides our retirement lifestyle coaches with insight into potential niche markets. Health concerns alone offer opportunity for practices focusing on nutrition, exercise and personal training, as well as support for health care in the many existing specific health conditions. Health concerns can be expanded into family health issues and relationship coaching, as people deal with their changing health issues—both their own and those of their family members.

Of course, these niches can be further segmented into women’s and men’s issues, or addressing the needs of the many in the “sandwich” position of dealing with aging parents and boomerang children.

The high importance placed on the opportunity to work, 61% in the survey responses, reflects a newly defining aspect of retirement life. This is driven by the fact that many retirees expect to have 30+ years of healthy life ahead of them. Not only do they want to supplement their financial resources to be sure they have adequate means to meet these long-term needs, but they also want to be busy, feel useful and be engaged in something they love to do.

Many retirees are returning to work in part-time positions especially created for them to capitalize on the expertise that companies cannot replace but also to permit the retiree the flexibility to engage in other activities. Others are setting themselves up as independent consultants, maximizing their skills advising the industry at large that they know from years of experience. Still others are turning hobbies into businesses or creating a business in an area in which they have always wanted to work.

What is evident from the responses to the Retirement Lifestyle Coaching survey on what constitutes quality of life in retirement is that the respondents place a high priority on issues such as personal growth and freedom, having a life purpose and making a contribution to the community and world in which they live.

Seminars that help these people prepare to retire are important to having a successful retirement experience. People who have a carefully thought out retirement lifestyle plan to complement their financial plan have a much higher chance of enjoying a fulfilling and meaningful life in their retirement years.
