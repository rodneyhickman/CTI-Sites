The Apocalypse Horsemen: Four Behaviors That Can Doom Your Relationships

by Fernando Lopez, CPCC

You may be doing them.

In this article, we look at four negative behaviors that happen in relationships. Why learn them? Because as you become more aware of them, you’ll also be better able to consciously stop them.

The four most toxic behaviors to a partnership are: criticism, defensiveness, contempt and stonewalling. They are so lethal to a relationship that John Gottman (relationship expert and best-selling author) calls them the Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse. They usually appear in the order below. As you read about them, mull over the way each horseman gallops (or doesn't gallop) into your relationships.

 Network Support Wizardry

Hi Everyone,

The topic of this month is “Discussions.” Please read through the information to refresh your memory.

Participating in Discussions

How do I add my creative PROMOtional idea, workshop or teleclass?
Once you are in the discussions area, click “PROMOs” to open the discussion thread. Then click the red link “Post a Message” and add your information in the text area. If you want to add special formatting, such as bold, italics, bullets, etc., you can use either the QuickEdit commands or regular HTML as you're typing it in. When you're ready, click "Post My Message." The page will reload, and your message will appear with the others.

How do I add a new discussion?
Once you are in the discussions area, click "Add Discussion" to begin a new discussion if you don't find an existing one that fits what you want to talk about.

How do I add a new discussion by email?
Send a new message to the community email address. You can get this email address from the "About This Community" page. Note that only messages sent from the same email account you are registered with will be accepted.

How do I post a new message?
Click on an existing discussion. To post a new message, either scroll down to the bottom of the page in a discussion or click the "Post a Message" button in the toolbar to go there. Type your message into the text box. If you want to add special formatting, such as bold, italics, bullets, etc., you can use either the QuickEdit commands or regular HTML as you're typing it in. When you're ready, click "Post My Message." The page will reload and your message will appear with the others.

How do I post a new message by email?
Make sure that the subject of your message matches exactly the name of the discussion it is referring to. Note also that only messages sent from the same email account you are registered with will be accepted.

How can I delete or edit a message I posted?
To edit or delete a message, click the "Edit" or "Delete" buttons next to your post. You can edit for only 30 minutes, to allow you to fix typos or reword something, but you can't edit after that. Use the "Edit" button to correct errors rather than using the "Back" button in your browser. If you use the "Back" button, your comment will be posted twice instead of edited. It's possible to delete a post at any time if you're a registered user.

Why are my messages being rejected when I reply to discussions over email?
You must send emails from the same email account that you used to register on the Co-Active Network. Also, if you are replying to an existing discussion, make sure that your subject line matches exactly the name of the discussion.

Thank you and have fun posting!

Enjoy!

 Ask Judith

Dear Judith:

Two weeks ago, my husband and I received the devastating news that he has advanced cancer and probably will only survive another six to eight months. As you can imagine, this has turned our lives totally upside down. Not only am I horrified at facing such a monumental loss, I also find myself angry with my clients who seem to have minor problems in comparison to what my husband is going through now. Most of the time, I can hardly concentrate on what my clients are saying because I feel like I am wasting precious moments that I could be spending with my husband. Do you have any thoughts on how to work when you are faced with a life and death situation in your own life?
—A.A.

Dear A.A.:
I am so sorry to hear about your husband. I can only imagine how hard this time must be for you. It sounds like the most important thing that you can do for your clients is to be honest with yourself about what your own needs are right now. Naturally, it’s hard for you to separate yourself from your own current experience. This is perfectly understandable, given how recent and shocking this news has been. I imagine that you are putting a lot of energy into self-managing around your clients. Given your current circumstances, I wonder whether this is actually beneficial for you. I also wonder whether your clients are consciously or unconsciously picking up on your anger, despite your best efforts to self-manage.
