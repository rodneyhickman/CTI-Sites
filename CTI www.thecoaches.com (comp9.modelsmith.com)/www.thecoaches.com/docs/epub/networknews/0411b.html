A Nice Cup of Coaching Tea?

Is it time for another Boston tea party? One that is upside down and back to front perhaps? This time, the tea comes pouring out of the waters of Boston Harbor and flows back into tea chests in the holds of the great trading ships. It's carried back to Europe to spread light, comfort and companionship in the Old World.

Perhaps it's time not just to ship the tea, but to take the tea bushes and transplant them from one continent to another. We old Europeans want to brew a warming cup of Co-Active tea for everyone, and we now need more than just the excellent teabags that come on ships from America.

It's time to take a great resource and let it grow freely in other lands and climates. It's time to feed the growing hunger for a stronger, more independent Co-Active community in Europe, in the spirit of loyal cooperation of friends and allies.

The other day, I coached a client where the issues that were coming up revolved around what is commonly called work/life balance. At some point, I asked my client to describe his ideal day. It consisted of the pursuit of a variety of leisure interests. Although he is in the process of changing the direction of his business into a new and, as he described it, more exciting direction, work did not feature in his ideal day.

When I reflected this to him, he said, "No, would yours?" Well, actually, mine would. After all, we work for long enough, so why shouldn't it get us into our fullest energy?

Later that day, I happened to be typing the phrase "work/life balance" and stopped to think about it a little more. Suddenly the phrase seemed loaded in that it implies that a fulfilled life somehow cannot include work. Work is, after all, a part of life, although for many it keeps them away from family and leisure pursuits.

When we almost automatically talk of work/life balance, are we unconsciously bringing in an assumption that "work" and "life" are to be kept separate?

I may feel like King Canute down at the beach, but from now on I shall be using the term "work/leisure balance." Anyone want to join me before my feet get wet?

Tony Stanley, Pinner, United Kingdom

Responding to Discussions
As a friendly reminder, please be sure to copy the text you wish to reply to or that you're referencing. If you have managed your account to receive digests, simply replying to an email in a digest will forward a list longer than Santa Claus gets in December. Please make it useful and to-the-point for the receivers.

Also, good to keep in mind that if you're responding to a discussion, is it really keeping that particular thread active and alive or did you just start a new one? Hmmm.

Private/Public Discussions
As a member of any given community, you will see the Private Discussion link in the left nav bar. (Geek speak for the green shaded margin on the left with all the links in it.) You will actually see both discussion boards.

As a visitor and not a member, you will see only the Public Discussion board. These two different areas are used uniquely by each community, with each deciding their use. Note: The Main Community has a Public Discussion area only. There is no Private Discussion board there.

Is What I'm Seeing the Truth?
Caching (pronounced "cashing") is where what you're viewing is actually historical in context. Let me explain. If you do not log out of the system, your page (link) will be cached. Every time I click on the Co-Active Network, I see the Main Community home page with 2,584 members. It's actually, as of this writing, 2,811. I must LOG IN freshly each time I visit the site to see the current live information.

Clean out your cache and cookies and you should start fresh. I do this by having my Internet browser open in front of me, then going to Tools>Internet Options>Delete cookies>Delete files and then I clear history. This will not be an issue when the Network gets its upgrade.

Do You Have a Road Runner Account (rr.com)?
If you seem not to be getting emails from the Co-Active Network and you have an rr.com account, please know that it's a known issue with RoadRunner; its server-side spam filtering is at fault. About the only thing you can do right now is to sign up with a different email provider. Sounds drastic, and, do it only if you're NOT getting the emails and want to.
