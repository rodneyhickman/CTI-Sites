How do you prepare for a client? Do a few Sun Salutations? Cordon yourself off from the kids and the dog? Make a nice cuppa tea? Sit in a special “coaching” chair? Or merely note the time, run to the phone and wing it?

As a new coach, I have gone the gamut on my rituals for coaching. Sometimes, I sit at my desk until two minutes before the client is to call, madly checking e-mails and feverishly surfing my yahoo. Or I’ve taken the thoughtful approach: light a nice, smelly candle, spend 15 minutes reviewing the client’s notes and homework assignments, and make a rough plan of the things I want to remember to cover (a.k.a. remind of late payment!). I’ve coached from my office, with the window overlooking tall trees, or from the leisure of the living room couch, or even sitting cross-legged on my bed, propped up with many pillows. I’ve been in my PJs, or full-on “hard clothes” and shoes. I’ve taken calls back-to-back or splat in the middle of a day of nothing going on.

What I’ve noticed is that really no matter what I do before the coaching, where I coach from, or how I prepare, what really counts is who I am being during the coaching. As soon as the coaching call starts, I must turn all my focus Over There…and that’s what makes the coaching work. I can tell if I haven’t shut down my own racing mind.

As coaches, we need to do whatever it takes to get to that grounded place, for the sake of the human on the other end of the line who pays us to be 100% there. It is a skill that I find takes practice and patience with myself. I can try to self-manage till the cows come home, but if I haven’t really gotten quiet in my mind, I’ll be fighting silently for the whole call.

So rituals may help…do what makes sense to loosen your grip and open the space in your head and your heart. Dance, open the windows, listen to your favourite song of the summer, review your notes, stand on your head, take off (or put on!) your PJs, and be your whole, present self when you say, “Hello client!”

Carol Faculjak, Life Insight Coaching, moved to England from Washington D.C., in 2004. She wrote this while sipping a nice cuppa tea. In her PJs.
