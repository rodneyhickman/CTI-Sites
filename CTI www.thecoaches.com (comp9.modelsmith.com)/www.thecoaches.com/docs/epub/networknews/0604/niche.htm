Co-Active Coaching for Health/Vitality/Wellness

I often get puzzled looks when I tell people I am a professional life coach who works in the area of weight loss. “What does life coaching have to do with a diet?” I’m asked.

And I love it when they ask that question! After all, they are being curious and I’ve got lots to tell them about the topic. I love the opportunity to educate people about a tool that goes way beyond the limited scope of a diet.

Here’s what I know: If we adopt a diet or an exercise plan, it works temporarily, while we are following it. Once adherence (as the fitness community is fond of calling it) waivers, the weight comes back, as if it’s been on a little hike and is coming home to rest. If we change deeper attitudes about food (and ourselves and even life!), permanent change occurs.

That’s what we’re all about in the Health/Vitality/Wellness niche community. The beauty of deep and lasting change that all life coaches experience with their clients marries a focus area of extreme importance: the client’s health, fitness, vitality and the quality of their future life.

As a “loser” who has lost more than 70 pounds permanently (defined as more than five years), I know my own personal change could not have happened without the deeper emotional, mental and spiritual growth that I accomplished while losing weight. I wish I had had a coach back then!

But, the small city where I live in the U.S. Midwest (Madison, WI), tends to be behind the coasts in terms of information and trends, and there weren't many coaches here 10 years ago when I began my quest for fitness.

Luckily, my clients across the country now have the distinct advantage a coach brings to their lives.

Using Co-Active skills to coach clients towards greater health, fitness and vitality is thrilling for those of us who work in this area. The organic nature of coaching addresses the entire human being. The result is deep and enduring change. The accelerated growth that occurs is as exciting for the client as it is for the coach.

In some ways, we might feel we work backwards from the Co-Active model. Small “a” appears first and can seem overwhelming for many clients. Many of them cling desperately to their little “a”s of losing 50 lbs., competing in a triathalon, or improving a health condition. The urgency they may feel over an illness or a medical condition can accentuate this desperation.

This is where creating a safe place for the client is so important. Often, the feelings that come up are ones they have never expressed or acknowledged before. Trusting me is one of the many gifts my clients give me every day.

The accountability tools for weight and fitness, like food and exercise logs, can reinforce the attachment to little “a” even further. But, believe me, Big “A” is always there, describing the road ahead, beckoning us to a greater, more magnificent way of being.

Clients quickly learn it’s a focus issue: if you focus on food, you get more food. If you focus on how much you hate exercise, you get more hate. If you focus on personal values and growth, limiting beliefs are smashed and lives are redesigned while bodies are re-sculpted.

Many of my clients double their salaries, find work they love, and develop romantic partnerships…all the wonderful life changing things that happen to each of your clients happen in health and fitness coaching, too. Losing weight or getting fit becomes a part of the picture, not the only focus.

The coach’s attachment to outcome is another Co-Active principal that can become sticky with health and fitness coaching. My experience as a seasoned weight loss coach and my own experience losing weight may inform me and suggest a course of action. However, I’ve always found that trusting the client’s resourcefulness is the most valuable tenet of Co-Activity in these cases.

I can’t tell you how many times I’ll be thinking in small increments (like adding fruit and vegetables to the daily eating plan) and the client will blow me away with a really big step like giving up sugar. Wow! In those cases, it’s easy for me to look back and see how I could have held my client small if I didn’t have the Co-Active model working for me.

So, if you’ve ever had a client, friend or family member who needs an expert coach in health and fitness, our community is full of talented, dedicated coaches. And, if you’ve considered the niche for yourself, I’d like to personally invite you to join us to learn more about this exciting coaching community!
