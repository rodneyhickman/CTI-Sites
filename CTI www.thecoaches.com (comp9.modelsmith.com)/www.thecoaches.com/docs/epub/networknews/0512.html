Rainbow Price Niche

When we started the Rainbow Pride Niche for gay, lesbian, bisexual, transgender, tran-sexual, two-spirit, inter-sex and queer folks, it seemed like a good idea to get the rainbow up and flying on the Co-Active network. I experienced some personal trepidation at “coming out” to the whole coaching community. I wasn’t sure that I was the right person to lead the GLBT community—what do I know? Who am I to be a leader of this community? I don’t even have “gaydar”—that inner radar that tells you when someone else is gay. People have to come out to me over and over—and usually I need my partner to tell me that “I’m part of the family” or something similar doesn’t just mean they are friendly. That said, I have been learning something new. I am new at Co-Actively “leading” a community. I am new to being out on the Internet. I ask myself constantly, “What is important for the queer community in Co-Active space?” But today I am pondering what is important for the Co-Active community about queer space?

 Member Musings

I lived for eight years in Paris, and I offer my services to coaches who are trying to make sense of some of the cultural and linguistic differences that exist and need to be honored between our cultures.

Let's consider how much we might learn from the unexpected reactions to coaching that we can be sure to receive if we work with French people. Most likely, we'll be asked to clarify concepts and principles abstractly, but more directly and succinctly than we might be used to. The French mind-set is very Cartesian, that is, logical and linear thinking are idealized...and people are judged by their ability to explain themselves using "le mot juste," the right word that exactly fits the occasion.

This said, the exact mapping and translation of terms is exactly what you want to withhold from your French clients. They want to understand concepts so that they can judge whether they like them or don't like them: whether they make sense to them or not, based on previously lived (or secondary and interpreted) experience.

 Network Support Wizardry

Dear All,

I thought I’d bring something back that has crossed all of our screens as we became members of the Co-Active Network. It’s information that often times goes unread and passed over quite quickly. Please take the time to review what you signed up for as a member of the Co-Active Network. The “trust” I spoke of earlier comes from this place.

After you entered the country you’re from on the registration form, this is what we all agreed to:

We are thrilled to have you join us in our endeavor to bring the magic and impact of Co-Active Coaching and Leadership to the world!

In joining the Co-Active Network, you agree to honor the following guiding principles:

* The Co-Active Network is a source for creating learning, sharing resources, cultivating alliances, developing opportunities, and building community among coaches and allies of the coaching profession.

* The Co-Active Network lives from a place of abundance. We come together to give to one another generously, knowing that in so doing, there will be more for all of us and the world.

* The Co-Active Network embraces and honors diversity of views. We celebrate differences and look with deep curiosity to learn from one another.

* Members of the Co-Active Network will fully honor and respect the intellectual property of their fellow members. Materials and ideas adopted from the Co-Active Network will be fully attributed to their creators and used by permission only.

* Communities in the Co-Active Network are self-governing. CTI's role is to attract the initial leaders and provide a basic structure for them to initiate the new community, and maintain guidelines for quality. The Community will be responsible from a stance of leadership for what it creates.

* We recognize that members of the Network will have a wide variety of backgrounds. To meet the needs of this diverse set of audiences, we accept that some communities may wish to open their membership only to those participants who share a certain level of experience, education, and/or set of credentials. These parameters are in no way intended to be exclusionary, but are designed to provide members with a peer group with shared purpose from which to build a committed community.

* The vision of the Co-Active Network is to support and champion Co-Active coaches and leaders to achieve their greatest impact in the world.

Peace,
Andrew Gabel

 Ask Judith

Dear Judith:

My client keeps asking me for advice and I know that we were trained not to give advice. What shall I do?
—E.R.M.

Dear E.R.M.:
So, it would seem that you, too, are asking for advice! That’s okay because this is an advice column. However, you’ll also notice that I always ask questions in my answers, as well. In my response, I invite you to see what is most helpful to you.

The first thing that I wonder when I hear your question is what is it that your client is truly asking for? Does he or she need facts? Is your client looking for a strategy?
Does your client think that you have some particular expertise from which he/she can benefit? All of these questions are great questions to explore with your client so that you can be clear about what is really being requested of you.
