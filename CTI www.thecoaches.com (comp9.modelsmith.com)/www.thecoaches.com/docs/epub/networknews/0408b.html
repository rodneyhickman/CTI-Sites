Welcome to the inaugural edition of the Co-Active Network News! As I begin to write this introduction, I find myself filled with awe and gratitude: awe at the fact that we are already more than 2,000 members strong and gratitude for all of the people who have made it possible. Upon completing CTI's core curriculum two years ago, I had a vision. I imagined a world in which each and every human interaction, whether occurring in a personal or vocational setting, would become infused with Co-Activity. In my mind's eye, I saw the transformation of entire professions—Co-Active teachers, Co-Active lawyers, Co-Active executives, Co-Active healthcare providers, Co-Active

Heroes
by Jamie McDonald

Even Gandhi had bad breath.
Oscar Wilde had moments when the right word simply escaped him.
Nelson Mandela must have had a few wobbles.
Churchill was a bit of boozer.
Edison must have come up with a few pointless inventions—perhaps a moose-comb,
and Jesus had a relationship with that woman.

Based on extrapolation,
the Queen presumably has a bottom,
and Johnny probably isn't always Rotten,
and Mozart must have fluffed a note or two from time to time,
and not all of Wordsworth's poems rhyme,
and Alexander Fleming discovered penicillin
because somebody got their microbiology wrong.

Even Gandhi had bad breath.

Hi All,

It's me, Andrew! I'm the man behind the green curtain, The Wizard of Support! I'm here to make your Network experience as smooth as possible. Each month I'll be bringing you this column with musings and helpful tips on how to navigate the Network with fun and ease.

I must preface this month's column by first showering the wonderful and fabulous Debra Bruce (Lawyers niche) with gratitude for composing most all of the following text to her community a short while ago. This message is timely and is in service to everyone. Thanks Debra!

"We" understand that some of you could benefit from knowing how to manage the avalanche of emails you are receiving from the Co-Active Network listservs. You can regulate the flow by using the Manage Account feature. Here's how:

Getting to "Manage Account"
Go to the Co-Active Network website at www.coactivenetwork.com. You will probably see your name at the top of the green column of boxes on the margin. (If not, you may need to log in.) The last line of that first box (the Welcome box) says "Manage Account." Click on "Manage Account."

Choosing "no mail" for all listservs
You will be taken to a page where you can choose "No Mail" if you do not want to receive any email at all from any of the Co-Active Network listservs (including this one). This would be good to use while you are on vacation so that the listserv won't get an auto-responder out-of-office message from you every time someone posts. (Quite annoying, especially if your message says you are vacationing in Tuscany or Hawaii or something.) It cannot be used for just some listservs and not others.

Choosing "no mail" for some, but not all, listservs
For the listservs you want mail from, click on the "Real-Time" or "Daily Digest" box opposite that listserv to put a check in the box.

For the listservs you don't want mail from, don't put a check in any box opposite the name of that listserv. If there is already a check in a box, you can remove the check by clicking on it.

Real-time vs. daily digest
For the Main List and each community you are a member of, you can elect to receive messages posted in real time, or you can get one daily digest that contains all the messages from that day in one long message or even both. For example, Debra chooses to get a daily digest for the Main List because it has a high volume of messages. However, she receives real-time messages from this Lawyers niche listserv because the volume is low and the discussion is more closely targeted to her interests.

Whole message vs. link
For the listservs that you choose to get real-time messages from, you can choose to get the whole message, or just a notification that there are new messages and a link to get to them on the website.

Help feature
If you are still confused, you can go to the Manage Account page and click on "Quick Help" near the top right corner of the page. There, you will find answers to frequently asked questions.

Save changes
Don't forget to activate your changes by clicking the "Save Changes" button at either the top or bottom of the Manage Account page.

Thank you for taking the time to review these features. We want to sharpen our communications in this highly communicative environment. Now go in and manage your account!

Love,

Andrew Gabel
Operations & Support Wizard
