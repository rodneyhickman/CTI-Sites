Please Don’t Call Me “Religious”
by Rev. Lydia Richards, Coordinator
Christian Coaches Community

Pamela, a coaching colleague, called me to chat about her Christian client. Pamela was confused. During a long intake, the client had shared that his Christian faith is one of his core values. At the close of the call, as Pamela mirrored back for her client the values she heard, she described him as a “religious” man. He harrumphed and stumbled and was clearly upset by this comment. The clock ran out. The call ended poorly.

This client is not unusual. For many Christians, being “Christian” and being “religious” are worlds apart. Many who follow the teachings of Jesus have grown uneasy with the connotations of being “religious.” Maybe even you. I know for my family, we rarely miss a Sunday service. We pray together and memorize scripture together, yet honestly, I would not say we are “religious.”

 Network Support Wizardry

This month, I’m going to follow up on a couple of posts I created recently. This information is important to repeat. Thank you, I know it’s useful to be reminded.

Replying to posts can create an unintended impact on almost 7,400 members of this community.

A common mistake is replying to a PROMO that is in digest form. You MUST open the post and read it in its entirety before you respond. Almost always (99.9% of the time), the post contains contact information for you to sign up or get additional information etc. Please take your time and thoroughly read the information before you respond too quickly for information that is already there for you. This has occurred recently to a few posts, with numerous replies filling our inboxes with single phrase quips of wanting the phone number or to “sign me up” or asking how to register.

I know there are exciting opportunities that many of you want to participate in. Let’s be mindful not to inadvertently reply to the entire membership for such requests.

Helpful hint: By clicking on the author’s name of the original post, you can get their contact information to get additional questions answered and/or requests addressed. Please contact them privately.

This phrase—“The item .1ab23456 does not exist, it may have been deleted”—will show up when you click a link that has been moved or deleted. Chances are, it was an opportunity that needed to be moved into the PROMOs thread. Give a look there to find it!

Reply to sender/Reply all is a feature that I’m still looking into adding to the public and members-only discussions area. CTI originally designed the discussions to default with “reply all” so as to keep the conversation open, alive and visible. Now that we’re at a size that warrants some fine tuning, we’re looking into adding these additional features. Thank you for your persistence in requesting this. I believe we’re able to accommodate. Stay tuned!

Dear Judith:

I heard that you used to be a librarian before you became a coach. Do you have any good book suggestions for enhancing creativity? I’m already familiar with Julia Cameron’s work. I’d appreciate any other suggestions that you have.
—K. M..

Dear K.M.:
Yes, it is true that I used to be a librarian and it is also true that I am a prolific reader. I love reading books as much as I love coaching. I’m interested in whether you want these recommendations for your own edification or whether you are going to suggest the books to your client. If you are reading these books to find ways that you can be more creative in your own life and with your clients, that is fine. If you are planning to assign one or more of these books to your client as homework, I think that you might want to do some prep work with your client beforehand.
