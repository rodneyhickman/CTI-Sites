Integrating Spirituality & Coaching
By Vince Gowmon, CPCC

Integrating spirituality and coaching is an increasingly prevalent theme within the coaching community. The most common question seems to be: “How do I bring the two together so my practice is a veritable reflection of my authentic truth?”

The answer lies in firstly realizing spirituality is not something concrete. It cannot be attained, learned or grasped mentally, and, therefore, it is not a process of adapting spiritual terminology, ideology and methodology into a coaching practice. Spirituality is a realization and expression of a deeper knowing, our unified field of consciousness. Spirit is immanent and, therefore, spirituality is a means of expressing that which is our essential nature. Spiritual coaching is our own unique way of answering the call of spirit and expressing its ineffable power through our presence, words and actions in the present moment, for the sake of supporting others to do the same.

Why am I getting someone else’s email digest in my in-box? Well, there’s an easy way to stop this. It’s not really their email digest, but a copy of it. And, we don’t know what we don’t know about how it will show up on the other end, so we simply hit reply. Let’s all post in a clean and thoughtful way. If you want to respond to a discussion, DO NOT HIT REPLY to an email digest. If your account is set to receive email in a digest form, to send a clear response, choose the red link, Post a Message, from the original thread.

Dear Judith:

My coach pretty much always gives me good homework assignments to do between our coaching sessions, but I don’t know how to do that for my own clients. Do you have any thoughts about what makes up a good homework assignment?

C.S.L.

Dear C.S.L.:

You’ve gotten me curious. What makes your coach’s homework assignments so powerful for you? I bet that you can begin to find some good answers there.

I imagine that what works about your coach’s homework assignments are that they are tied to something that supports your passion and that they create learning. I find that the most powerful homework assignments emerge from what my clients are yearning for. The key to creating homework assignments with impact is to be listening not only to what your client is saying but to what they are not saying, as well. Homework is a great way to begin to give space to that which we haven’t yet voiced.
