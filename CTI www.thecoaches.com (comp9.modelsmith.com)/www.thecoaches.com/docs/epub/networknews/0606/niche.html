Spiral Dynamics Integral Niche

I overheard a conversation the other day. It sounded something like this:

Mary:
Hi, John. I see you signed up for the Spiral Dynamics Integral niche on the Co-Active Network.

John:
Yes, I’ve been in the niche for over a year now.

Mary:
I haven’t been there as long as you, only four or five months, and I saw the first email posted for the group last week. What’s the deal? Is this niche doing anything at all?

John:
Not much as far as I can tell. I’m really curious about this Spiral. I’ve had several conversations with other coaches who are really excited about it. But I don’t know much about Spiral Dynamics Integral and very little has happened inside this niche.

Occasionally, an email like the one you mentioned gets posted that again exclaims the excitement and buzz about Spiral Dynamics. And there was a bit of activity when the niche formed a year ago. But for the most part nothing has happened.

Mary:
So I am really interested in this subject, John. Do you have any suggestion about how to leverage this niche to support my interest?

Before John could answer I had to interrupt their conversation and introduce myself.

Hi. My name is Cherie Beck. I am an organizer for the SDi niche on the CoActive Network. I am sorry for eavesdropping, and my ears were burning so I couldn’t help myself.

I am so pleased to hear that you are talking about Spiral Dynamics, and yes, this niche has been slow to gain momentum, yet the interest in the topic just seems to grow.

I recently returned from a conference sponsored by the Edges Coaches Community, where both Don Beck and I presented the basics of Spiral Dynamics Integral and its application to coaching. It was a tremendous success and the coaching aspect is really beginning to take shape. For the past year, many of us have been deepening our learning and using our clients to experiment with, and it seems like we’ve been able to squeeze enough juice now to attract the attention of many more coaches. Even Don Beck, the originator of Spiral Dynamics (popularized from the work of Clare W. Graves), who had very little personal experience with life or executive coaching, could clearly see the value. In fact, at the Edges conference, we added another stream from personal coaching to this newly emerging wave (somatics, embodied leadership and wisdom), as taught by the Strozzi Institute, into the mix and the results were really exciting.

John:
I really would like to know more about how Spiral Dynamics Integral and coaching work together, Cherie. What are you learning about it?

Cherie:
That’s a difficult question to answer in a sound byte, John, because there is so much to point to. In service of offering a bottom line, let me make this statement: a significant advantage of learning Spiral Dynamics Integral is the perspective it offers us to coach from. This perspective helps us see and hear into our clients, their agenda and their lives in ways no other perspective I’m aware of does. Think about how medical professionals assisted in the health and wellness of clients before we had microscopes, MRIs and laser technology. Spiral Dynamics Integral helps us to see smaller subtler causes deep within the landscape of our clients’ lives and businesses, and then to match the appropriate resources and skillful means like laser beams to meet the coaching situation. Spiral Dynamics Integral will deepen, expand and target your coaching like nothing I’ve come across this far, while at the same time utilizing or underlining all what we know and have mastered thus far.

Mary:
That sounds like a very powerful platform to coach from.

Cherie:
I’ve certainly found that it is.

The challenge early on remains, however, to commit to learning enough about the basics of Spiral Dynamics Integral in order to participate, contribute and practice the rich complexity found within it. So, in part, the lack of momentum experienced in the SDi niche was due to efforts toward developing pathways into the model in smaller and smaller chunks, and pathways out for those who do invest in the Spiral Dynamics model and seek to apply it more directly to their clients.

Your conversation, Mary and John, is so timely because there are several new offerings launching over the next several months to help the 200 members of our niche take smaller steps, choose smaller investments of time, money and thinking to build a knowledge base incrementally from which we can have meaningful dialogue through the Co-Active Network virtual platform.

Mary:
That sounds great Cherie. Where can we find out more about what will be launching?

Cherie:
There are two places. First, over the next week or two, watch for additional postings through the SDi niche. Second, I am requesting you also sign up under www.thenextwave.us. By adding your name to this list, you take another “baby” step toward investing yourself in pathways to learning the Spiral and augmenting your coaching practice. It also will help indicate how many of the people registered as members of the SDi niche on the Co-Active Network are actively interested in the topic.

Let me add, my conviction that the field of coaching has a major role to play in the unfolding of the Spiral has grown stronger over the last year. If you are interested in this topic at all, I assert that the master Spiral intelligence within you is at work. Trust me, that intelligence is smarter than we are!  It will lead you to your own fulfillment, for its function is to help us adapt to the life conditions we live in.

We in the Spiral Dynamics Group seek to live the “prime directive” of the Spiral and create a world of Thrive and Help Thrive. Sounds like ripe conditions, sweet and juicy for coaching, don’t you think?!

Cheri Beck, CPCC, is co-organizer of the Spiral Dynamics Integral Niche.
