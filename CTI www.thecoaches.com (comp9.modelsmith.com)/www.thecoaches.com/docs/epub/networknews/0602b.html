 Member Musings

Success by Association

“The greatest genius will never be worth much if he pretends to draw exclusively from his own resources.”
—Johann Wolfgang von Goethe

I have a confession.
I was a geek in grade school. I was that nerdy girl wearing the polyester "floods" from Pic’n Save. I kept my nose in books like Jane Eyre or Lord of the Rings. Small talk baffled me. Then in the sixth grade, two popular girls adopted me. They took me to the mall and taught me how to dress, became my friends and included me in their activities. Soon I was learning how to make friends and flirt and hold my own with the "cool" kids (politically active, articulate, popular, musically hip) I admired. What happened? These two girls pulled me up to their level of social finesse by association. And these girls were great people—smart, socially aware, responsible and compassionate human beings. People I admire to this day. Our values meshed. And they knew something I needed to learn about social rapport.

Hello Electronic Device Users!

Replying to emails can make a mess in members’ in-boxes. Please take a quick moment before you decide to reply and consider whether you want the whole community to see your “possibly personal” post or whether you want to send it to the individual that created the post. Please know that our system is currently configured so that the entire community receives the posts and replies. If it’s not provided near the title of the post, you can find the individual’s personal email address by clicking on that person’s name and getting it from her/his profile. And please remember never to reply from a digest email.

PROMOS have been getting posted outside of the public discussion thread provided. Please remember that any post that sells services and or products must be posted from the PROMOS thread found on the Main Community public discussion board. If you are offering a FREE information call that sells or promotes additional fee-based services or products, these must also be posted from the PROMOS thread. Please be respectful. I’ve heard from some members who are concerned that their post’s title won’t show since the title is taken up by the thread name PROMOS. After you click into the PROMOS thread, after you click “post a message,” simply type the title in the text area and leave a line or two to have the content follow. It will show up quite distinctively. I assert there are some examples in your in-box right now that have a clear distinction from the thread title.

Calendar events enroll more when the name(s) of the presenters are posted in the event description. I receive inquiries often as to who are the presenters. I don’t know either.  Please remember to add this information.

Hot topic: Muting Your Phone! Attending Main Community Events sure is cool, huh? To maximize the learning, it is imperative for all callers to mute their phones unless speaking or asked to speak from the presenter. It is not fair to the presenter to consistently ask callers to mute their phones while extraneous sounds are occurring. If you do not already have an external mute button or feature on your phone, please test *6 on your phone with someone. While *6 may mute some phone systems, it is not a guarantee. You may “think” you are muted when in fact you are not. Some of the wacky sounds we’ve heard just today and last week are nose blowing, a gaseous member whose burp resonated for a couple of moments, a plethora of outdoor sounds, kitchen sounds (need I say more?) and even a hushed expletive. As you can see, I am passionate about giving the respect to presenters that they deserve.

Respectfully,

Andrew

 Ask Judith

Dear Judith:

My client always wants to focus on talking about his past. He does have some goals for the future, but he’s more interested in using events that have occurred in the past to explain either why it is hard for him to get things done now or why he is currently failing at achieving his goal. I keep pointing him toward his future but he seems stuck in the past. What can I do to help him change direction?
—V.J.

Dear V. J.:
This is a prime opportunity to apply a lot of curiosity in your coaching. Clearly, your client has an attachment to past events. Rather than assume that it’s necessarily a bad thing, try changing your perspective and imagine that his “past” is now being relived in his present. What if there is a helpful message that these events are trying to communicate to him?
