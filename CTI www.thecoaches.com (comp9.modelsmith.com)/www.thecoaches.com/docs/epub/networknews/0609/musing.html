Boston Co-Active Geographic Community
by Joanne LeBlanc, CPCC

You know, our Bostonian accent is unique, just like we are. “Paak the caa in Haavid yaad” (translation: park the car in Harvard yard). You might ask yourself, “What happened to the letter ‘r’?” and if you asked a Bostonian, we would either say nothing at all, or with a sidelong glance remark, “What aah?”

My name is Joanne LeBlanc, and it is my pleasure to invite you to Boston, Massachusetts, (and to the Boston Co-Active Geographic Community).

Three years ago, when I began my CTI courses, I attended a number of meetings held in person in Boston and met many wonderful coaches committed to making a difference in the world. I found these group meetings rich, encouraging and energizing. Little did I know that within three years, I would find myself stepping up to lead this community.

I consider a large part of my role to be reawakening this community. Personally, I have a deep desire to explore how we can raise the bar here in Boston. We have more 140 coaches signed up for our geographic community. What’s possible when we pool our resources for the sake of each other, our clients and our community? Can you imagine the energy level of a community that is reawakened?  

That is exactly the energy you’ll find in the Boston Co-Active Geographic Community these days. We had our first “Think Tank” call in August, beginning the process of brainstorming and fleshing out what this community wants to create and how it chooses to create it. We will hold a second “Think Tank” call in September to accommodate coaches who were unable to attend our first call. The plan is to resume our in-person meetings in October! I am committed to engaging as many coaches to join us as possible. If you have not signed up yet and would like to become a member of the Boston Co-Active Geographic Community, please go to the Co-Active Network website, click on geographic communities and hit Boston.

For those who have not experienced it yet, Boston is unique. Besides the distinctive Bostonian accent, it boasts a rich culture and history. With all the amenities of a big city, including wonderful restaurants and entertainment, Boston maintains a very intimate feel. For sporting fans, Boston is the home of the World Champion New England Patriots and the Boston Red Sox. (And Boston sports fans? We’re about as dedicated and passionate as you can get. Did somebody say fanatic?)

We also have one of the largest and most active ICF chapters in the United States, with more than 350 members. ICF-NE is celebrating its 10-year anniversary this year. (http://www.icfne.org/index.php). And now CTI has joined the party by offering its courses in Boston. How much more fabulous can it get?

Come play and explore in Boston. If you have already completed your courses, how about assisting in Boston—to expand your experience and continue your learning? Great opportunities await you in Boston! The more the merrier, I say, especially when it comes to a community of coaches!

Joanne LeBlanc, CPCC, is the leader of the Boston Co-Active Coaching community, a graduate of CTI’s Leadership Program, founder of Life Coaching by Design (www.lifecoachingbydesign.com) and co-founder of the Coaching Tree (www.coachingtree.com). You can reach her at joanne@lifecoachingbydesign.com.
