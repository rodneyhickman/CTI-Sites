Dutch Treat
by Joke (say “Yoka”) Botman, CPCC

Dutch Treat has a whole new meaning now that the Co-Active coaching courses have been introduced in the Netherlands! A strong and vibrant community is starting to bloom in Holland (like a beautiful tulip), full of enthusiastic coaches who got acquainted with the Co-Active way of coaching. Here is what an experienced coach had to say after the class: “I am more conscious of my personal behavior in coaching, less serious. I will dance more and rely on my power and skills.” And a woman new to the coaching profession: “All will be different. It is like starting from zero. Through intuition, I will use my heart more and my mind less.”

Beginning this year, Dance2Move (a company of my business partner Wim Vermaak and me) is bringing the CTI courses to the Netherlands and Belgium. In January and in March, we had a Fundamentals course, and from April through July, the first intermediate series is taking place. After the summer, the second series is planned.

The Netherlands has a number of coaching schools, as do most countries in the world. However, Co-Active coaching is the first ICF-accredited, in-person coach education to be offered here, and, of course, we are very proud of that.

We are also very keen on supporting the ICF in the Netherlands. Right now, the ICF is not as established as we would like it to be in Europe as the representative of all coaches in the world. We hope the ICF, with the bond between CTI and ICF and the accreditation, will grow strong in this part of the world and be recognized as the leading body for certified coaches. Companies have a great need for a reliable accreditation; they know they are hiring a “real” and well-educated coach when s/he has an accreditation. Bringing the first ICF-accredited in-person coach education to the Netherlands and Belgium is a big step forward in reaching that goal. We are very excited about that.

Most participants of the courses are Dutch, but we do have some people from England, Germany and Norway. The participants are so very excited about Co-Active coaching and about the fantastic leaders; being an “older” Co-Active coach, it is heartwarming to see this excitement. (I do encourage everybody to assist at a course, because a few years after your own education, you pick up a whole lot from the back of the room!)

A new feature is that we started a private community on the Co-Active network, the Dutch Co-Active Student Club, which gives students a place to communicate with each other and ask all kinds of questions to the organizers. Telephone group calls will be one of the ways to connect (and get used to the certification calls).

After students “graduate,” they can join the Dutch Co-Active Club, which is a gathering of all Co-Active coaches living in the Netherlands (and may be a native English speaker, and there are many of them) and Dutch Co-Active coaches living abroad. The Club has not been very active yet in gatherings. It is always good to know of each other, and know where to find a colleague trained in the same philosophy.

For me, personally, it is great to see this Dutch Co-Active community expand. I started my training in 2001 in England, and in 2003 became the first Dutch CPCC living in the Netherlands. And sometimes that felt lonely. So watching all these great people join up is a tremendous joy. By the way, have you noticed, too, that people who participate in the Co-Active coaching courses are so interesting and driven and great fun and sincere—just about the nicest people you would want to meet anywhere. I feel very grateful and look forward to a sparkling Co-Active future for coaching in the Netherlands and Belgium!
