Five Conversations on the Frontiers of Leadership
David Whyte

Leadership gets a great deal of emphasis these days, though as a poet I often ask myself if there is a better word, or a better avenue of enquiry than putting all of our eggs in that one basket of exploration. Leadership dominates our conversations in organizations—large, small, entrepreneurial, or academic. I am often asked: "What is the secret of leadership?" as if it were a static piece of information rather than a dynamic relationship between an individual and the world. Some believe that if you simply adopt certain traits—being decisive, taking bold risks, or articulating a clear and compelling vision of the future—you will have the very stuff of leadership. But do any of these commonly observed traits make up that magic ingredient that invites others to join or follow an individual? It is my opinion that they are not enough by themselves. They are isolated diagnostic characteristics that a given leader may have, but to my mind they are not the heart of the matter.

I'd like to start off by saying, Thank You! to all of you who have been vigilant in managing your email accounts on the Network. Seems we're getting better and yet you can count on me to hold the bar high on this one. The trick, I assert, is in remembering all the places your email is connected to on any given day, especially when you're trying to tie down all those loose ends before you go away from your computer for a weekend getaway, business trip or extended vacation. Well, the Co-Active Network is one of those loose ends to tighten up before you leave. This seems to show up across the board and mostly with our corporate friends who enable "auto reply" on their email accounts. Please remember to go to "Manage Account" and select "no mail" or simply un-check all the boxes to the right of your communities in which you belong. Helpful Hint: Put a sticky note on your computer screen as a structure to remember that the Co-Active Network is impacted by your auto replies.

You may have noticed that some of your discussions that promote services and opportunities have disappeared on the Main Community discussions list. Don't panic, I've moved them to the Promos thread. This is now the place to post all of your fantastic opportunities. If you're selling, you're promoting. Simple eh? This allows the receiver to choose. A much better impact indeed!

Ever been one to look for a bridgeline for a specific event on the Network? Here's a couple of tips for you. First, go to the event description posted on the calendar. It may have been added to the description closer to the date of the event. In some cases, it's posted there at the time the event was created. Second, if it's quite a bit of time before the event, say five days or more, you may need to simply wait and see if you receive an email from the organizer closer to the date of the event. And third, this is usually saved for those desperate, last-minute, "Oh my gosh, what's the number?" times: send an urgent email to the organizer of the community or post a discussion in the community of origin. Let me explain this last piece. Most all events except "Main Events" are generated in a particular Niche or Geographic community. Post your urgent plea to the community of origin. There is a link at the bottom of the event description to take you there. It looks like this: "This page is a pointer to this original event."
