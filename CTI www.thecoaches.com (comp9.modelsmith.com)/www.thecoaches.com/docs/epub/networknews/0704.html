Hello Friends,

Have you sprung into action? This is the season for birth and renewal. The metamorphosis of life continues its miraculous cycle. What are you birthing this season? How will you transform into your most beautiful self? It may take a season, a year, a decade. Who are you becoming?

Think of this: who is the future self you are today that your child or young adult would have longed to be or to meet? Who would you have yearned to know and love? A colleague of mine shared this concept of looking at myself today as the future self I have become and am today. Pretty cool, huh? Of course, I have a future self who is loving me and creating me today for all of the tomorrows that lie ahead…ready to meet me, there. I love to ponder this as I crack my current eggshell into another stage of life. The mystery of it all, along with conscious choice, brings life, transformation and excitement. Keep blooming!

We’ve Got It All in Northern Alberta
by Laurel Vespi

“Words do two major things: they provide food for the mind and create light for understanding and awareness.”
—Jim Rohn

One day in the summer of 2002, I walked into the world of Co-Activity and haven’t looked back. It was a venture into all the things that could possibly be dreamed, imagined and attained. It was a step into the arms of a community that supports, encourages and challenges its members to not settle, to look past mediocre, to reach for what you want. Over the past five years, the look and feel of my coaching business has changed, but what has remained consistent is my commitment to the concept of co-activity and community.

Back in 2002, there were few coaches in the greater Edmonton area, let alone CTI-trained ones. In those days, we scurried back to Vancouver for courses and looked to their community for support. Lately, though, Co-Active coaches seem to be springing up everywhere in our backyard, and so it seems timely to create a Northern Alberta Co-Active Community.

 Ask Judith

Dear Judith:

I have been working with a client for the past year. We have a great coaching relationship and I really enjoy working with her. The issue that has suddenly come up for me is that there is an opportunity for me to meet her in person. We have worked solely by telephone during our entire coaching relationship, and I am suddenly very nervous about meeting her in person. I guess that I have built up a picture of her in my mind and I don’t want reality to change it. What are your thoughts on this matter?
Sincerely,
—J.V.

Dear J.V.:
It is not uncommon to have a long and satisfying coaching relationship by phone and to never meet your client in person. It is also true that many coaches and clients do decide to meet one another in person. This can occur prior to beginning the coaching relationship, during the coaching relationship or after their coaching relationship has ended. I have coached many people and never met them just as I have coached people for long periods of time and then met them in person.
