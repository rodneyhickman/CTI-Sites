Piercing Chronicles
By Sandi Davis, CPCC

I have a pierced belly button. Not an unusual thing at all these days. Except I'm 42…42 and overweight…42, overweight, with no intention whatsoever of showing my belly to the world. What, you may ask, does a 42-year-old woman with a decidedly soft (OK, fat) tummy want with a belly piercing? Am I trying to recapture my youth, kind of a delayed teenage rebellion? Am I hoping a piercing will make me cool and hip rather than middle aged and boring? Was I drinking at the time, or did I lose a bet? No. I got the piercing because I really, really wanted it. Have for a really long time. Years in fact! I got it because it's fun and I wanted to stop putting off fun in my life until I got a better body. I got it because I am tired to my bones of trying to appease the unending choirs of critical inner judgments and criticisms of my body and what it deserves. Truly, I got the piercing as a way to create a relationship with my body from a place of acceptance and beauty (with maybe a bit of adolescent rebellion thrown in).

 Network Support Wizardry

Hello Summer Vacationers!

It’s that time of year again to be especially diligent with managing your email while you are away from your office. We are seeing an increase in bounced emails to the site informing us that you’re on vacation, or out of the office or both!

Please place a reminder in your personal calendar to make sure to change your email delivery options on the Co-Active Network the day before you depart.

Be sure you’re logged in and then click on the Manage Account link in the upper left of the navigation bar. Once there, go to the Suspend Email section near the top of that page. Click the “No Mail” check box and then be sure to save your changes. You will not receive any email from the communities to which you belong.

Now, be sure to also place that reminder note on the day of your return, to revisit the Manage Account section and un-check the “No Mail” check box and save your changes to once again start receiving emails from the Co-Active Network.

Enjoy your fun in the sun!

 Ask Judith

Dear Judith:

I have a client who is a writer. He is usually pretty diligent about getting his work done but recently he’s been taking a lot of time off. Instead of writing, he’s been visiting friends, playing ball and spacing out watching television. He feels awful about himself when he does this. How can I motivate him to go back to his writing?
—M.M.

Dear M.M.:
Ironically, the best motivation may come from encouraging him to keep doing what he is doing. What if your client has been working too hard and these diversions are much-needed mini-breaks? It may be possible that he has been too focused on his work of late and his fun and recreation needs are requiring some more positive attention. Respite can often be a helpful response to a stressed out situation.
