A Vision for Change: Teachers as Coaches Leading the Way
by Dr. Linda Lea

The other day I was on the treadmill at my health club and glanced up to see a handsome young man with a dazzling smile looking at me. He ambled over to where I was, winked and said, "I just had to say hello to my favorite teacher." The shocking realization of who he was came quickly as I struggled to maintain my composure and slip into my teacher mode. He was one of my former students of over 20 years ago. The interaction that followed was delightful as he shared with me his success in both his professional and personal life. I realized that to him, I was still his teacher, and I couldn't help but reflect on the huge impact teachers have on their students' lives. I would like to think that who he is today is partly due to the contribution I made as his teacher a long time ago.

Vancouver Coaches

This fall marks six years of Co-Active community building in Vancouver. We have been pioneers in developing skills, sharing resources and supporting one another since October 1998, when our first dedicated CTI coaches, Terry Ramsey and Juhree Zimmerman, cooked up the idea to deepen our learning through monthly meetings.

We've had some significant milestones in the local coaching community. When the 5th Annual ICF Conference was held here in October 2000, it was a strong contingent of CTI coaches who contributed to the successful planning of the event. In November 2000, our local ICF chapter, with many CTI coaches at the helm, became the first ever to legally incorporate.

Vancouver's coaching community has spawned:

  • CTI leaders Patrick Ryan and Juhree Zimmerman
  • ICF President-Elect Steve Mitten
  • Two web sites: www.vancouvercoaches.org and www.vancouvericf.org, both of which have active member directories
  • 84 CPCCs in the Vancouver region, and the number keeps growing
