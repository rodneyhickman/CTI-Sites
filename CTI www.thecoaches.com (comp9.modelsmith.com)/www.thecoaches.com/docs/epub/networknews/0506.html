Spiral Dynamics? What?

Co-Activity...Co-Active Coaching can change the world! OK, so lots of us believe this. But how? Are there instructions? How about a map? Our community myths say “one person at time.” Will that be enough? Don’t we need to have an impact on communities and organizations and countries, too? And how about all the other people we know are doing similar work? How does it all fit together? Are we a vehicle for something major that’s happening developmentally in our world? What is the consciousness that is expanding through us?

Models...maps, do you love them the way I do? I’ve tried to figure out what makes them so compelling to me and I think it’s that, since everything seems connected to everything else through my eyes, I need help distinguishing one piece from another. I need some context to put the pieces in for them to make sense. The world is complex and getting more complex all the time. Who talks about this stuff? Where can I have this conversation? Especially, where can I have this conversation with others in the Co-Active Coaching community?

A Cottage Industry
Let me share a story from another industry and another time. In 1730, cloth making was a cottage industry throughout England. Many individual weavers were scattered around the country creating cloth for their clients. Over the next 50 years, a series of technical revolutions would sweep through the industry. First, John Kay's flying shuttle in 1733 started things, quickly followed by Crompton's mule which produced large and cost-effective quantities of fine, strong yarn.

As a result of these changes, by 1800, the number of workers needed to turn wool into yarn shrank by 80%. From 1780 to 1812, the cost of making cotton yarn dropped 90%.

Dear L.A.:

In my experience, celebrities are people first and celebrated people second. It is easy for coaches to get swept up in the celebrity's persona and lose sight of the person behind the story. This is especially true in this era of idolizing or demonizing celebrities and separating art and politics from our everyday life. Don't confuse the celebrity with the person. In my own minute brush with "celebrity," I have become very aware of the power of projection. Once one is seen as a public figure, all sorts of expectations get projected onto the person and, although this happens regularly with non-celebrities, it is a force that must definitely be dealt with to a much greater degree by people in the public eye. Don't ignore the celebrity aspect of this person, but don't get seduced by it either.
