The Dream Whisperer

“To dream or not to dream, that is the question…”

Recently, I found myself caught up in a heated discussion about the value of dreams. A fellow traveller equated dreams with delusions. I preferred to view them as the source of inspired being and action that lead to a fulfilled and joyful life. This debate and an earlier conversation with my coach prompted me upon waking one day to consider my role as a “dream whisperer.” Is coaching like horse whispering?

Horse whisperers are known for their magical, intuitive communication skills with horses. They display thoughtfulness, gentleness, sensitivity, perceptiveness, confidence and quiet strength to have a successful relationship with horses. They are grounded yet dance in the moment, mirroring the mood and energy of the horses to build trust. We even have horse whisperers within our CTI community, such as Ann Romberg and Rebecca Hourston. They provide inspiring role models.

Hello,

In this month’s column, we’ll be visiting some topics that will continue to keep us neat, clean and impactful. Sound good? I thought so.

Promos are a wonderful way to get your products and services out there to the greater community, and it creates great visibility. Please remember that when you’re posting your product or service to the community that you “post a message” under the Promos thread in the Public Discussion thread listing. I’ve been seeing promos posted outside of this thread lately. Please do the community a service and keep it easy. Selling belongs in the Promos thread. Thanks!

Daily Digests have taken on a whole new look and feel. Have you noticed? Please remember: do not reply to the digest as a whole. Boy, is that messy in my inbox! I’ve been hearing some great feedback regarding the ease of reading the digests now. We’ve received some wonderful assistance on the re-design of the digest format. The amazingly talented and gifted Fernando Lopez has contributed to the Co-Active Network once again. He was one of the main contributors in terms of functionality design and testing when we first began the Co-Active Network. CTI will forever be graced with his impact and contribution. Thanks Fernando! (He is also one of the organizers of the Relationship Coaching Niche.)

Bridgelines are sometimes a bit challenging to find when it’s that time to get on the call and participate in some hot topic. First, go to the community that is sponsoring the event and click into the event. Sometimes it is posted in the event description closer to the date of the call. Second, if it’s not there, go to the “About This Community” in the left nav bar and see who the organizers are. Click on one of them to get their contact email address and maybe their work number to see if they have the number for you. Third and lastly, contact me at networksupport@thecoaches.com. I may not always have the number, as the communities use other services for their bridgelines outside the ones that CTI provides.

Technologically yours,

 Ask Judith

Dear Judith:

I have been a coach for seven years and I am embarrassed to admit that my honeymoon with coaching seems to be over. When I first started coaching, I really loved the work but now I feel like it is no longer fun or exciting. I feel trapped and angry. What should I do?

R.M.

Dear R.M.:

Reading your question, I felt that you could have just as easily been writing about a seven-year romantic relationship. It seems to me that what you are really wondering about is how to keep a long-term relationship fulfilling and vital. Just as you must put energy and attention into the needs of a romantic relationship, you must also pay attention and invest energy in your relationship with coaching.
