Systems Coaching Integration Now Accredited by the ICF
In early July, the Systems Coaching Integration course (formerly “Dojo”), the fifth course in the Organization and Relationship Systems Coaching (ORSC) curriculum, was accredited by the International Coach Federation for 18 continuing coaching hours. Now the entire ORSC curriculum is accredited for a total of 84 hours!

Systems Coaching Integration: Moving Toward Mastery is the newest course in the ORSC curriculum. The three-day advanced course provides a learning space to deepen and hone the skills of Organization and Relationship Systems Coaching. This course integrates the dimensions of Relationship Intelligence, System Geography and Relationship Path through hands-on practice, group supervision, a deepened understanding of the underpinning of the model and practice, practice, practice.

With the accreditation of Systems Coaching Integration, effective August 1, 2006, CTI revised the ORSC course packages to include the Systems Coaching Integration course, and we’ve scheduled the course in many more locations. Here is a list of upcoming Systems Coaching Integration courses:

Learning from the Masters: A Path to Success for Solopreneurs
“Of all the challenges of being a solopreneur, one of the most challenging is isolation. By definition, solopreneurs work alone. If you’re not careful, you can start to believe you are alone—completely on your own in your quest to envision, design and implement your business,” says Dr. Joelle Jay, CPCC, who is an executive and leadership coach, speaker and author specializing in leadership and personal effectiveness. Read more about what she has to say about solopreneurship.

The Enthusiastic Solopreneur: Tools for Maintaining Motivation
Janet H. Moore, JD, provides executive coaching and consulting to lawyers, and international lawyers in particular, through International Lawyer Coach, Inc. In this article, she says, “As Ralph Waldo Emerson quipped, ‘Nothing great was ever achieved without enthusiasm.’ The most successful coaches seem to have enthusiasm in spades, and this enthusiasm propels their coaching careers—and their clients—towards greatness. Sometimes the isolation of the solopreneur life can threaten a coach’s enthusiasm. If this happens to you, recapture it with some of these strategies.”
