Letter from the President

March has blown into Northern California with its chilly winds and promise of spring. The cherry trees outside my office are beginning to bloom once again.

This year, 2010, is beginning to bloom as well with new opportunities and new possibilities. The economic shake up of 2009 has caused us all to re-evaluate our priorities, bringing clarity about what is most important. This is true at CTI as well. As 2010 opens its wings, we are also opening to a deeper commitment to the transformative foundation of Co-active Coaching.

At the heart of it, Co-active Coaches are change agents in human consciousness, focusing at the level of core identity: who people are and how they perceive and interact with their world. It is our belief that, whatever their walk in life, people long to evolve as human beings and continually grow into more expansive, aware and integrated human beings. Whether in a professional coaching relationship or in combination with some other profession or model, the heart of Co-active Coaching is to serve this evolution.

With our individual clients, inside organizations, in education or the non-profit sector and with our families, Co-active Coaches all over the world are making this particular difference and contributing to the unfolding story of humankind, one designed person at a time.

However you are using the Co-active Coaching model, you are a part of this flowering. Thank you for your partnership in spreading our work in the world.

With gratitude,

Karen Kimsey-House
President, CTI

Looking for Buried Treasure

Did you know there are hidden gems all around the Co-active Network? If you follow my simple instructions, I'll help you find a special cache of goodies just waiting to be uncovered. You may end up doing some digging, but this digging isn't in the dirt.

Go to www.coactivenetwork.com and look at the green navigation box at the left edge of the screen. Login if you are not already. Take 10 steps north…no, wait…it's not even that hard. Hit the link labeled "Resources." Depending on the community you are in when you start, you might find anything from sample forms and community event recordings to transcripts and book lists. You'll find articles, photos and tools to boot.

Use this method to explore the 39 Niche Communities and to connect with other coaches who share your interests and challenges. These folks may not be buried, but many of them are treasures. Scout out opportunities to connect personally and professionally through the 27 Geographic Communities that reflect the global presence of coaches. The relationship potential here is priceless.

If you didn't find what you were looking for, then it's time to get some help in the form of our search function, which is available in the upper navigation area on every page of the Co-active Network. Hit that "Search Site" link and load it up with your keyword. Click the search button. Whew! That's a lot of loot!

Or you can narrow your search to a specific area, such as a particular discussion board. Navigate your way to your target location and then click the "Search Site" link. Type in your search term and then choose the option to "Search This Location," which will confine the results to the area you are in on the site.

My trove of treasure metaphors is now empty. Please let me know if there is something you'd like to learn how to do on the Co-active Network.

As CTI has grown and expanded, it has become a challenge to remain in open and personal relationship with our various community locations. We have been looking for a way to effectively serve and communicate with our population in locations where we hold courses. In the words of Karen Kimsey-House, "Our relationship with our community matters. It's the most important function of our business."

The Ambassador Program is a very real and tangible way for CTI to show its commitment to specific locations and the people who work Co-actively there, and to generate conversation around Co-active ideas and activities. Ambassadors have been chosen for their understanding of the transformative nature of the work that we do. Their work will reflect what is most important to us. Their mission is to represent CTI when CTI can't be there to represent itself.

This program is Karen's key initiative for 2010. As on-ground representatives in local markets, these ambassadors will be in direct relationship with Karen. The ambassadors are not only local representatives of CTI's voice, but also of Karen's voice.

Because the Ambassador Program is such an important initiative for CTI, we are taking time and care to build the program slowly. Strategy and design of this program began with the New Year, following on the planning and recruitment work completed in late 2009.

Thus far, three representatives have been chosen to work with Karen in establishing the structure of the program, which will expand by an additional three cities in Q2 or Q3 2010. The vision is to eventually place an ambassador in all of CTI's North American locations.

We've moved our west coast Leadership Program location for Leadership tribes beginning January 2010. The Sequoia Retreat Center is located in Ben Lomond on California State Highway 9, about 10 miles north of Santa Cruz and an hour's drive from the San Jose airport. The breathtaking setting and human history associated with this venue add special energy to the Leadership Program experience.

The Sequoia Retreat Center is a place of reflection and dialogue sitting on land that has been held sacred since 1949. It is a place where love has been experienced, pain has been transcended and hope has been born. People from all over the world come here to reflect on, renew and reenergize the meaning and purpose of their lives and work.

Sequoia Seminar was incorporated in 1949, when the building of the lodges and cabins found there today began. The first lodge, "Casa de Luz" (House of Light), combined the traditions of community working together and the goal of blending architecture with the land. "Las Alas de las Aguilas," Wings of the Eagle, was built by hundreds of people as a "barn raising." Each stone in the fireplace was signed by the person who placed the stone.

In the decades since, extraordinary things have happened here. Men and women from Armenia and Azerbaijan met at Sequoia to explore ways to end their war over Nagorno-Karabakh. At Sequoia, Israeli and Palestinian leaders held a week-long seminar that produced a working document for peace titled Framework for a Public Peace Process: Toward a Peaceful Israeli-Palestinian Relationship.

The Sequoia Retreat Center stands on the shoulders of peacemakers, reconciliation makers, and stewards of the land and Sequoias. The Center's roots are in the people who have created extraordinary lives. The Co-active Leadership Program honors this remarkable and inspiring legacy through the continuing training and support of global leaders.
