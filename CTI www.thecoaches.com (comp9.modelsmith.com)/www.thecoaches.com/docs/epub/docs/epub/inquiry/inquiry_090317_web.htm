Greetings Co-Active Community:

This morning, on my way in to the office, I was bathed in a sudden cascade of blossoms from the flowering trees. Spring has come to Marin County!

It's not that I hadn't noticed...it's just that I hadn't NOTICED! I hadn't really taken the time to s-l-o-wwwww down, breathe in the freshness and really connect to the rhythm of the earth. I'd been much too busy paying attention to what was happening with the economy, with my country and in the world. I'd been going here and going there (a recent fabulous trip to Amsterdam), meeting wonderful people and having incredible conversations. I'd been focused and intentional. I'd been COMMITTED.

Meanwhile, nature was doing its thing. The days were getting longer, the buds on the trees were turning an unbelievable shade of green, and the things that flower were getting all geared up for the big "Yee Hah!" that is Spring.

I realized that days had passed since I had taken the time to really savor the beauty all around me and the great, good gift of being alive. I decided that it was time to cultivate a good case of "Spring Fever" and roll around in the grass with a good book and a bottle of wine. Or just sit and watch the fog roll in over San Francisco Bay.

Yes, these are "turbulent times," as the media loves to say, and each day there is fresh news about the challenges that we face. Yes, I do believe that it is a time of transformation and powerful change, and we all need to step up.

Paradoxically, it is wildly important, at the same time, that we not take ourselves too seriously; that we continue to cultivate the ability to laugh at the ridiculousness of being human. It's vital that we take care to appreciate the seemingly small things that make a life so rich: the way the air smells after it has rained, the golden color of the light at dusk, the magical shimmer of a full moon as it floats in the night sky, and too many other details to ever list.

Whatever our portfolios are doing and whatever happens on Wall Street, we are wealthy beyond compare, bathed in the beauty of the world around us and blessed with riches of experience, heart and love.

Wherever you are as you read this, pause for a moment and find something to chuckle about. Find something clever or amusing about yourself that is funny and charming. Now let your eyes wander and rest on something beautiful. You probably won't even need to get up out of your chair--beauty is that near at hand.

Me, I'm tickled by my own foolish self-importance and am looking at the blue, blue Bay outside my window. The fog will be along at any moment.

Blessings,
Karen

Karen Kimsey-House

Lost in (Virtual) Space

A common question that lands in my mailbox at networksupport@thecoaches.com is "What happened to my Daily Digest emails from the Co-Active Network?" It is a reasonable question. But we're dealing here with technology and that's rarely reasonable. The path from the server that hosts the Co-Active Network to your email inbox is treacherous and fraught with dangers for the Daily Digest. Here are a few suggestions that will increase the odds that the Daily Digest will successfully complete the journey.

Because the Digests are an automated email, it is possible that your email service is blocking them on your behalf. The larger service providers (AOL, Yahoo, Gmail, Hotmail, etc.) change their filtering rules on a regular basis, so what was getting through a few days ago may now get caught in a net you didn't even know was put up. Also common are spam filters that gobble up messages from the Co-Active Network. Missing your Digest? Check your settings.

To help make sure that your email service provider or spam filter is not screening out messages that you want to receive, add these "From" email address for the daily digests to your address book: Public_Discussions@coactivenetwork.com
members-only_Discussions@coactivenetwork.com

If you have the option, set your spam filter to accept all email from coactivenetwork.com. You may need to contact your email service provider or search their help files to learn more about how to improve the odds that Co-Active Network Daily Digests reach your email inbox.

Environment and Awareness

By Catherine Walker

CTI Is Now a Green-Certified Business

Who would have guessed that our humble beginnings as a small committee aiming to make a sustainable difference within the CTI office would shepherd the company through a rigorous process to become green-certified by a program that spans nine Bay Area counties? It's official. CTI is now a green-certified business through the Bay Area Green Business Program, joining more than 1,700 other green-certified businesses.

In fact, CTI activities exceeded BAGBP's basic requirements. For instance, we recycle and re-use course materials like bandanas, chimes, fans, markers, leaders' course outlines, and packing and shipping material. That makes many of our long-standing processes green, too. More recently, all student documentation for our Leadership program is handled electronically through Launchpad and i-Cohere technology, saving CTI approximately 8,664 pieces of paper (or 17.3 reams) in 2009. Look for the "Bay Area Green Business" logo, which will soon begin to appear on materials produced by CTI.

We welcome your ideas and suggestions for making CTI even greener in 2009--please contact Catherine Walker, our Sustainability Coordinator, at 415.526.1652 or catherine@thecoaches.com. To find out more about the Bay Area Green Business Program, visit http://www.greenbiz.ca.gov/AboutUs.html.

Maria Cunningham, who originally introduced the green initiative to management, found inspiration in the Green MBA program at Dominican University, near our office in San Rafael, CA. If you are looking for help in "greening" your organization, please contact them. Dominican's mission statement reads: "Dominican University of California's MBA in Sustainable Enterprise is an engaging learning community where people with strong environmental and social values develop effective leadership capabilities to advance economically successful, ecologically restorative, and socially just initiatives in any type of organization." Visit www.greenmba.com or phone 415.458.3737 for more information on supporting your company's journey toward green. | top

Global News

Growing the CTI Family

In mid-December, Karen Kimsey-House and Sabrina Roblin travelled to South Korea to conduct leader auditions in support of CTI partners Ken and Susan Gimm in South Korea and Igshaan Soules in South Africa. More than a dozen candidates from those two countries participated in the auditions.

It was an experiment in support of the growth and development of these partners, and the process went really well. This approach allows the partners to grow their business as quickly as possible using local talent. Karen and Sabrina were impressed with the candidates and chose one leader from South Africa and six from South Korea.

Karen noted "it is always interesting to hold auditions in a different culture, and this was no exception. Auditionees were so vulnerable."

Auditions were held at CTI Korea's beautiful new building in downtown Seoul. They have a big vision for Co-Active Coaching and how it might support the growth and expansion of their country. Seoul really bustles, and the local Co-Active Coaching community reflects that.
