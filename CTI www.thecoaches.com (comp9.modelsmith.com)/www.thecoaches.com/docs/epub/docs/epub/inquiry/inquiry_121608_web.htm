Greetings Co-Active Community:

I hope that you are all well and warm this holiday season. What a fascinating time to be alive! Along with all of you, I have been following, with great interest, recent world events: the stock market doing a swing dance, unemployment climbing to dizzying heights, and our bastions of financial security reporting in with an interesting combination of "ooops" and "ummm...help!"

In some way or another, I imagine that we have all felt the impact of these recent events. It is true that times are a bit tough. However, along with the challenge, current circumstances represent an ENORMOUS opportunity. I feel strongly that a window has opened in the listening of the world. It’s clear that something has not been working. I can sense people everywhere, inside the business world and elsewhere, reaching "Okay, if not that, then...WHAT?!!"

As our President-Elect Barack Obama expands on his message of hope and collaboration, whichever side of the political aisle you are on, it is clear to me that it is time for each of us to step up. It’s time to take ourselves and our message seriously and stand in the courage of our own convictions. It's time to value and respect the inherent wisdom that lives at our core. It's time to lean IN to our own humanity and the humanity of others. And most of all, we must remember that every speck of life on the planet belongs to each other.

We will not be able to resolve the challenges that face us from our current paradigm, and hooray for that. It will take the combined creativity of us all. The current circumstances are neither "bad" nor "good." As always, they are just there. The variable is what we will create FROM those circumstances. Who will we each choose to become in the matter? I know that I feel fully "called forth" by the current situation, both personally and in my leadership of CTI.

As I write these words, I feel the strong resonance of this community. In the year ahead, we'll be looking at how to serve you more fully and bring you tools that you can use for greater impact and success. You are an essential part of CTI's message and without you, our work would lie dormant and fallow. For now I just want to say "thank you" for who you are. Your heart, caring and creativity is important. You matter.

Much love to all,
Karen

Karen Kimsey-House
President

Living La Vida Leadership

Typically when you hear about Co-Active Leadership, the focus is on aspects of the training program. Leadership calls its participants to be living in the moment, present in their bodies, self-aware and grounded in the place. Oh, and what places they are! If you’ve never experienced our Leadership retreat centers, let us introduce you to them.

Wildwood Retreat Center is located in Northern California on 210 acres of unspoiled forests, meadows and streams. It is home to deer, raccoons, hawks and wild creatures living undisturbed in their natural habitat. Redwood groves, fir trees and giant oaks grace a magnificent landscape of rolling hills, sunny meadows and striking vistas. A sanctuary for personal transformation and growth, this retreat center is a majestic, inspiring place full of natural contrasts and variety, breath-taking beauty and simple grandeur.

The Bend of Ivy Lodge is cradled in the Blue Ridge Mountains of Western North Carolina. The lodge--formerly a tobacco ranch--is the state’s first eco-conscious, carbon-neutral retreat center in the western region, nestled on 63 acres of land that slope gently down to the Ivy River. A grassy glade of walnut and sycamore trees along the river, views of the high Blue Ridge Mountains, and forest trails lined with trillium, jack-in-the-pulpit and rhododendron restore the spirit. Beaver, fox and great blue herons call this home. Surrounded by a lush natural environment with forest trails, a pond, waterfall and gardens, this lodge provides a serene and peaceful escape.

Situated 30 minutes south of Barcelona, Spain, Almiral de la Font is a luxuriously restored masia (Catalan manor house) perched high on a hillside in the Pinedes wine region surrounded by the Garaf National Park. With dramatic sea or mountain views from every room and terrace, this tranquil haven occupies more than 300 acres of private grounds with vineyards and olive groves. Explore miles of private trails to find pear, cherry, walnut and almond trees, in addition to many naturally growing herbs and vegetables. This extraordinary house may be within 10 minutes of the lively beach resort of Sitges, but Almiral de la Font is a world apart.

Environment and Awareness

By Catherine Walker

CTI Goes Green

One of CTI's six core values is sustainability. We asked ourselves, how can we more fully champion this value as a worldwide business?

CTI's management team--inspired by staff--decided to embrace the idea of becoming a Green Certified Business through Marin County’s Green Business Program. A Sustainability team was created, using staff volunteers, to determine what approach would work best within our company culture, establish objectives and brainstorm ideas.

The team explored what was involved in meeting the Green Business criteria, and over the spring and summer, completed our required water and energy audits. By fall, we were ready to dive into the next layer of transformation--bringing all of our staff and leaders on board to individually explore our ecological footprint and look at ways each of us can minimize our impact. An abundance of ideas bubbled up and were captured on a hallway white board for ongoing implementation.

Our Sustainability team rolled out a three-month plan over the last quarter of 2008 to introduce the three corners of sustainability--reduce, reuse and recycle--so that each aspect could be defined then introduced over time for each person and department. The results have been terrific! So far, we've exceeded criteria expectations each month and are well-prepared for our "Green Business" certification audit walk-through in January 2009.

We invite you to join CTI in our pledge to go green. | top
