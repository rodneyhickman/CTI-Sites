Co-Active Network Tech Tips

By Marcia Norris

Spring Cleaning

This season is all about growth and change, which makes it the perfect time to update your Co-Active Network member profile. Have you accomplished anything recently that would enhance your bio? Tell us in your profile. Update your progress through the CTI curriculum. Get the word out. Oh, and ask someone to take your photo so that you can add your lovely face to your profile. Come on! You know you want to!

It is very easy to edit your profile. Simply go to http://www.coactivenetwork.com and log in using your email address and password. Then click the “Edit Profile” link near the top of the green box at the left edge of the screen. Make any changes you need then click the “Save Profile” button. Don’t forget that last step because if you don’t click the “Save Profile” button, your changes will be lost.

Niche Communities and Geographic Communities

The Co-Active Network is a great place to connect with other coaches who share your interests and challenges. Do you focus on a demographic niche that you share with other coaches? Explore the Niche Communities list to find out if others are discussing topics that are relevant to you. If not, suggest a new niche by emailing networksupport@thecoaches.com.

There are also opportunities to connect personally and professionally through the 25 Geographic Communities on the Co-Active Network, which reflect the global presence of coaches. If you are interested in starting a Geographic Community in your area, let us know by emailing networksupport@thecoaches.com.

Managing Daily Digests

If you are active in several communities on the Co-Active Network, you may find yourself on the receiving end of many daily digests. Did you know you can turn off the daily emails from individual communities?

Login to http://www.coactivenetwork.com then click on the "Manage Account" link near the top of the green box at the left edge of screen. Here you will find a list of communities you are joined to under the heading "My Communities." You will see two columns of boxes, one labeled "Daily Digest," and the other labeled "Real-time Notifications."

If you do not want any email from a community, uncheck both boxes to cease email delivery for that community. If you want a summary of each day's activities sent to you once per day, check the box for "Daily Digest." (This is the default when you join any community.) If you want to receive an email as soon as somebody posts or responds, check the box for "Real-time Notifications." Finish by clicking the "Save Changes" button.

If you are traveling and want to temporarily halt all daily digest emails from the Co-Active Network, check the "Suspend Mail" box on the "Manage Account" screen. This will stop all email from every community you are joined to until you return and remove the check in the box. Don't forget to click the "Save Changes" button to make your changes stick.

If you have questions or issues with these or any other Co-Active Network technical topics, please email networksupport@thecoaches.com to ask for help. | top

Internal Co-Active Coaching

Are you working as an executive or organizational coach--or want to become one? CTI has launched its newest coach training program, Internal Co-Active Coach, created specifically for individuals working as internal coaches inside organizations as well as external executive coaches. CTI's Internal Co-Active Coach program builds a solid foundation of Co-Active coaching skills for professionals to apply in their role as an internal or executive coach. The Internal Co-Active Coach is based on CTI's industry-leading coach training curriculum and is specifically targeted for individuals who want to enhance their expertise at enabling companies to increase their human development and retention within organizations. This blended-learning program incorporates three interactive workshops, three learning labs, 1:1 coaching supervisions and access to CTI's interactive "Internal Coach Toolkit."

The program will launch in San Francisco and attendees include both internal and external coaches representing companies such as: Google, Schwab, U.S. Navy and Juniper Networks.
