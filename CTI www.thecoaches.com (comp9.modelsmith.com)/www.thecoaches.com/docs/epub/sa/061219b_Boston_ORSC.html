Co-Active Organization and Relationship Systems Coaching
Boston, MA, metro area
Course dates: February 17–18, 2007

The two-day Organization and Relationship Systems Coaching Fundamentals is an introduction to relationship systems coaching. It's an advanced-level course designed to introduce you to a coaching specialization that will provide you with new marketable skills for your practice. Become a relationship coach with skills for coaching business partners, teams, groups, boards, families and/or couples. These skills are applicable to all relationships and will enable you to specialize in a number of vibrant niche markets or expand your current role in organizational settings. For more information about CTI’s Organization and Relationship Systems Coaching curriculum, visit www.thecoaches.com/relationship.

In this Organization and Relationship Systems Coaching Fundamentals course, you will:

  • Discover the power of alignment coaching skills.
  • Learn how to create client awareness of the "third entity"—the relationship itself.
  • Practice coaching the "third entity."
  • Develop new skills that you will use to adapt your intake process specifically for relationship coaching.
  • Gain awareness of, and skills to use with, the behaviors that blight relationships.
  • Learn and practice skills and principles that can help relationships flourish.

The cost of this Organization and Relationship Systems Coaching Fundamentals course is $495.

This is the first course in the Organization and Relationship Systems Coaching curriculum, and it is a pre-requisite for the remainder of the curriculum. In order to be eligible for this course, you need to have completed the Process course with CTI, or have been through another ICF-accredited coach training program, or be a PCC or MCC. If you are in an allied or related profession (mediators, organization development specialists/consultants, mental health professionals such as therapists and family counselors, or consultants experienced in addressing the relationship dynamics of groups/families/teams), you may take the course if you have completed 18 hours of coach training through CTI (Co-Active Coaching Fundamentals) or through another ICF-accredited coach training program. The course provides 12 ICF CEUs.
