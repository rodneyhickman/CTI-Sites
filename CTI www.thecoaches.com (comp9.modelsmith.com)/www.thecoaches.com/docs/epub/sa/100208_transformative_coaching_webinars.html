Co-active® Coaching
TRANSFORMATIVE COACHING WITHIN ORGANIZATIONS

What is it that sets you apart as a Co-active Coach with your clients within organizational settings? As coaches who have been trained in the Co-active Coaching model, we are dedicated to our clients' growth and fulfillment in ways that are not "run-of-the-mill." Our intent is that we have an impact that goes from merely "good" to "extraordinary." At our best, we are deeply in tune with our client and bring courageous presence to the coaching conversation...even when it is difficult.

In this webinar series, we'll explore several challenges that coaches may face working with leaders in organizational settings and how to remain steadfastly committed to going "above and beyond."

These webinars are presented as a series, and we encourage you to attend all of them as a series. However, if you are not able to attend all of them, attend them one at a time. Between webinar sessions, we hope you’ll continue the conversation in a related discussion on the Co-active Network.

To register for the series, you must register FOR each webinar individually.

Webinar 2:
Transformative Coaching in Organizations: Shake it Up!
Date: Tuesday, March 16, 10:00 a.m. Pacific Time

Co-active Coaches get uncomfortable when the status quo seems to be in charge, and sometimes it's our job to "shake things up." We believe that for Co-active Coaches to create shifts for leaders in organizations (and anyone else!), they must be willing to -- in fact, intentional about -- rocking the boat. Sample topics in this webinar include:

  • Transformative learning and change comes from a DISORIENTING DILEMMA. Our job is not to help clients be more effective navigating the status quo or making gradual improvements to the way things are. Our job is to SHAKE IT UP! Co-active Coaches enable clients to shatter the very ground they stand on -- to question the status quo, to illuminate restrictive ways of viewing and operating in the world, and to construct more powerful and expansive ways of being.
  • Who must the Co-active Coach be to foster this type of transformative change with clients?

Webinar 3:
Transformative Coaching in Organizations: Standing in the Fire
Date: Tuesday, March 30, 10:00 a.m. Pacific Time

For Co-active Coaches, getting the job done is more important than looking good, having clients like them, or being viewed as a star coach -- this means that at times the Co-active Coach is willing to "Stand in the Fire" -- to create discomfort, to risk failure, all for the sake of the client’s transformative growth. Webinar topics include:

  • Shrinking back from the fire -- the things we cling to, such as approval, being liked, being seen as competent, having good ideas, staying comfortable. What’s the cost?
  • What does it mean to "stand in the fire?" Confronting our own saboteurs.
  • Three winning strategies: 1) Grow an appetite for the uncomfortable. 2) See the gap and your clients' ability to rise to brilliance. 3) Tell the simple truth.
  • Implications for you as a Co-active Coach.
