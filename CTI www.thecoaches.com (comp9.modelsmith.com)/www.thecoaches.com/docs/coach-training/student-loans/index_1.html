SLM Financial Corporation Student Loans

Whether you are looking to become certified and build a coaching practice or simply looking to develop new skills, we recognize how important it is for you to have the resources available to invest in yourself. To make the investment easier for U.S. students seeking financial assistance for CTI training, we now have Student Loans available through SLM Financial, A Sallie Mae Company.

For students enrolled in courses held in California, Colorado, or Minnesota, loans are available through the Career Training Loan Program at SLM Financial, a Sallie Mae Company. Please contact CTI at 1-800-691-6008 for information or if you have questions about the online loan application process.

Student Loan Information

Company Overview

Sallie Mae Financial Corporation is a wholly-owned subsidiary of SLM Corporation, a Fortune 500 company also commonly known as Sallie Mae. Sallie Mae provides subsidized government loans to students attending Title IV schools (traditional 4-year colleges). Sallie Mae Financial Corporation was created to provide non-government-backed/alternative loans for non-traditional schools such as CTI.

Type of Loan Offered

Career Training Loan

Borrower Requirements

Borrower must be a U.S. citizen, a U.S. national, or a permanent resident and must be creditworthy. You may be enrolled full, half time or less than half time. If you have no credit or a poor credit history, you may still qualify for a loan by applying with a creditworthy Co-Borrower.

Location of Courses

    » In-person courses held in CA or MN (Fundamentals, Coach Training, Leadership)
    » Any tele-course: Certification Program

Loan Amount

Tuition + additional funds to cover educational expenses (up to 60% of the tuition amount).

What Are Educational Expenses?

    » Airfare & hotel accommodation for in-person courses
    » Tele-courses: phone equipment, course phone calls
    » Certification Program: A personal coach during the 25-week program, books

Minimum Loan Amount

$1000

Maximum Loan Amount

Student may borrow up to the total cost of the education, less other financial aid received.

Interest Rates

Interest rates are variable (monthly) and based on the Prime Rate plus a spread. Your interest rate spread is determined by your and your Co-Borrower’s (if you use a Co-Borrower) credit history. The interest rates start at Prime +1% with fees as low as 2% for borrowers with excellent credit.

List of Lenders, Interest Rates, and Fees
Interest Rates and Fees available SLMA website. Look under “Private Student Loans” and click on “Career Training Loan.” Call SLMA at 1-888-272-5543 for Lender Information.
Repayment Terms

    » Repayment begins 30 days after disbursement. The standard term is 15 years with minimum payments set at $30 a month per loan. You have the option of making interest-only payments while in school and there are no prepayment penalties. After 6 consecutive payments, you may apply for up to two deferred payments a year. Final approval is at the discretion of SLM Financial Corporation.
    » After you have made the first 24 consecutive on-time scheduled payments of principal and interest, you may apply to have your Co-Borrower released. Student borrowers must meet applicable credit requirements at that time.

Penalty for Early Repayment?

No

Method of Disbursement

SLMA will disburse tuition funds directly to CTI via EFT. If the borrower requested additional funds, SLMA will disburse these funds by check to the student.

Funding Timeline

Funding in as little as 7 days or less from receipt of completed application. CTI recommends obtaining funding one month before course start date. Please start the loan application process at least one month before course start date.

Loan Application Pre-Approval

    » Within 24 hours of receipt of completed application, SLMA will notify CTI if loan application has been approved, denied, or if student may reapply with a Co-Borrower.
    » If loan application has been approved, SLMA will email Loan Application, Promissory Note, and TIL Disclosure Statement to CTI. CTI will contact and forward documents to student for review and sign-off.
    » If your loan application was denied, SLMA will notify and send letter to Borrower.
